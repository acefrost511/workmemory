## 摘要

Despite increasing interest in using Generative Artificial Intelligence (GenAI) in education, little is known about how students with disability engage with GenAI tools, and whether GenAI can support their learning agency. Drawing on dialogic feedback theory and expansive learning theory, this study examines how Year 8 students with disability in an Australian secondary school used GenAI to support their learning in a series of design tasks. Findings indicate that students with disability actively negotiated GenAI's role in their learning, often resisting full automation. They engaged in critical dialogue with GenAI, used it as a sounding board, and developed a nuanced understanding of its capabilities. Implications for inclusive education and responsible GenAI integration are discussed.
