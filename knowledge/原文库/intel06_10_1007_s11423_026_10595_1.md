## 摘要

Students often struggle to stay engaged and effectively regulate their learning when studying in online environments. This study investigates how learning analytics combined with generative artificial intelligence (AI) can support online learners' self-regulation skills. The researchers developed an AI-powered learning analytics system that tracks learner behaviors and provides real-time, personalized feedback to help students set goals, monitor progress, and reflect on their learning strategies. A quasi-experimental study was conducted with university students enrolled in online courses. Results indicate that students who received AI-generated learning analytics feedback showed significant improvements in self-regulated learning behaviors compared to a control group. The findings demonstrate the potential of integrating generative AI with learning analytics to create scalable, personalized support for online learners, particularly in contexts where instructor feedback is resource-intensive.
