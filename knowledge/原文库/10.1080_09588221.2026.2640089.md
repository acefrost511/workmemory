## 摘要

This study explores the use of electronic portfolios (e-portfolios) for learning Chinese as a second language, focusing on instructional design principles and their impact on learner engagement. E-portfolios serve as digital repositories that enable learners to document, reflect upon, and showcase their language learning journey. The research examines how systematically designed e-portfolio activities can enhance learner engagement, self-regulated learning, and Chinese language proficiency. Findings indicate that well-structured e-portfolio implementation, informed by instructional design principles, significantly improves learner motivation and reflective practice in Chinese language education.
