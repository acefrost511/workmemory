**标题**：An Empirical Study on Conversational Pedagogical Agents Promoting Programming Learning Performance Among Secondary School Students

**期刊**：电化教育研究

**卷期**：2026年第3期

**作者**：傅骞，赵亚宁，甘甜甜，郑娅峰

**关键词**：大语言模型；编程学习；教学智能体；对话式教学策略；学习表现

**英文摘要**：

Programming learning, as a crucial component of information technology curriculum in basic education, directly influences the development of students' computational thinking and problem-solving abilities. With the advancement of large language model technology, conversational pedagogical agents offer new pathways to support personalized and real-time responsive programming learning. However, existing agents predominantly operate in passive response mode, lacking intelligent guidance for students' cognitive processes and thus failing to effectively facilitate deep learning. Therefore, this study aims to explore the differentiated impacts of various conversational pedagogical approaches in middle school programming learning to construct more pedagogically adaptive agent support models. Based on the conversational pedagogical theory, the study developed two types of conversational pedagogical agents: the guidance-oriented type and the inquiry-oriented type. Through three experimental groups, the guidance-oriented group, the inquiry-oriented group, and the integrated group (the guidance-oriented + the inquiry-oriented), the study compared the instructional effectiveness of different agent application strategies. The results show that compared to the single-strategy groups, the integrated group demonstrates significant advantages in improving students' programming performance and problem-solving abilities, and more effectively stimulates students' medium-to high-level cognitive inquiry and construction behaviors. Based on these findings, the study further proposes three empowerment principles for GAI-enabled programming learning: shifting from technology-oriented to cognitive adaptation-oriented approaches, from fixed interaction to human-machine adaptive regulation, and from cognitive support to expanded non-cognitive support.
