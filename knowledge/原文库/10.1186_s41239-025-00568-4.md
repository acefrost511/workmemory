## 摘要

Advances in technologies which use Generative Artificial Intelligence (GenAI) to mimic a person's likeness or voice have led to growing interest in their use in educational contexts. However, little is known about how key stakeholders (teaching faculty and professional staff) perceive and intend to use these tools. This study investigates higher education employees' perceptions and intentions regarding the use of synthetic avatars (alternatively known as deepfakes) through the lens of the Unified Theory of Acceptance and Use of Technology 2 (UTAUT2). Using a mixed-methods approach that combined quantitative survey data (n = 173) with qualitative text response, academic stakeholders demonstrated a relatively low intention to adopt these technologies (M = 41.55, SD = 34.14). Stakeholders identified potential benefits including enhanced student engagement through interactions with historical figures, improved accessibility through voice synthesis, and reduced workload. However, they expressed significant concerns about exploitation of academic labour, institutional cost-cutting leading to automation, and degradation of human relationships in education.
