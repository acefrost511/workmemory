## 摘要

This commentary argues that teachers today operate under a persistent assumption: their primary role is to transmit curricular knowledge shaped by standardized assessments. Influenced by psychologized models, constrained by overcrowded classrooms, and tasked with an ever-increasing workload, many educators adopt dualistic views that separate mind from body and emotion from reason. Generative AI (GenAI) marks a decisive turning point for education. By taking over the routine labor of content delivery, it exposes how narrow the teacher's role has become and opens new possibilities for meaning-making, connection, and growth. It also accelerates a broader shift that destabilizes long-standing assumptions about assessment, authority, and what counts as "knowing" in educational systems. To teach is to participate in living systems of relation in which understanding emerges through coordination among people, tools, histories, and ideas.
