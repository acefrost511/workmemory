## 摘要

Although the demand for English academic writing talents is growing, teaching in China faces challenges such as students' lack of self-regulated learning, self-efficacy, and learning motivation. Therefore, this study aims to explore the impact of AI-assisted peer feedback with DeepSeek on Chinese EFL undergraduates' self-regulated learning, self-efficacy, and motivation in English academic writing. The researchers conducted a 16-week experiment with 60 third-year undergraduates majoring at a university in northern China, who were divided into an experimental group (EG, N=30) receiving DeepSeek-assisted peer feedback, and a control group (CG, N=30) receiving traditional teacher feedback. Mixed-design ANOVA results showed that both EG and CG students showed an upward trend in motivation, but EG students made greater progress than CG students. In addition, paired sample t-test results indicated significant differences in self-regulated learning, self-efficacy, and motivation before, during, and after the intervention, with effect sizes ranging from medium to high. However, the overall sample size was small. Semi-structured interviews further verified the quantitative research findings, which were considered to be more helpful than traditional teacher feedback in improving different levels of self-regulated learning, self-efficacy, and motivation. Implications and recommendations are proposed for future study.
