## 摘要

This study aimed to develop and validate a scale for assessing deep learning approaches in their own context among university students in the context of generative AI tools. Most existing measures were originally designed for traditional educational settings and do not reflect the substantial changes introduced by AI practices. To address this gap, a 16-item scale was developed across four dimensions—search meaning, relating ideas, analysing evidence, and attention concepts—after adapting items for AI-supported environments. The scale was administered to 765 Damanhour University students with prior experience using AI. Data were examined through exploratory and confirmatory factor analyses, convergent and discriminant validity tests, and network analysis. Results indicated strong psychometric properties and reliability at high levels. Network analysis revealed clear interconnections and highlighted central nodes representing core aspects of learning, which shaped other behaviours and reinforced adoption approaches. Overall, findings confirm the scale as a modern, reliable tool capturing students' strategies in AI-enhanced contexts, offering implications for future research and improving higher education practices in light of ongoing digital transformations.
