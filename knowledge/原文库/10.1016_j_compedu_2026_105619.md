## 摘要

Generative AI can augment diagnostic learning, yet the impact of explanation style on novices' reasoning remains unclear. We evaluate two styles—technical (evidence-/probability-based) and analogical (case-based)—in a randomized controlled trial with 60 third- and fourth-year medical students who diagnosed twelve de-identified acute abdominal pain cases. After an initial judgment, participants were randomized to one of three conditions (technical, analogical, or control [no AI advice]) and received ex post AI advice (or none in control) while thinking aloud; outcomes were diagnostic accuracy, confidence calibration, and cognitive load analyzed via mixed-effects models and protocol analysis. Both AI styles improved accuracy over control; analogical yielded the highest accuracy and the lowest cognitive load, whereas technical produced the best confidence calibration but increased load. Positioning explanation style as a design lever for AI-assisted learning, we show how design choices translate into generalizable knowledge for diagnostic training and decision calibration.
