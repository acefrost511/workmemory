## 摘要

With the growing integration of artificial intelligence (AI) in education, conversational AI agents are increasingly being deployed as teachable agents to support student learning. This study investigates how students interact with generative AI teachable agents and how these interactions shape students' cognitive and metacognitive engagement. Drawing on conversation analysis and learning analytics data from 120 secondary school students, findings reveal five distinct interaction patterns ranging from passive reception to critical negotiation. Students who engaged in critical negotiation demonstrated significantly higher learning gains and developed more sophisticated understanding of the subject matter. The study highlights the importance of designing AI teachable agents that scaffold productive human-AI interaction patterns.
