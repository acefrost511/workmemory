## 摘要

This study investigates how generative artificial intelligence (gen-AI) interacts with pre-service teachers' emotional labour and self-management in Australian initial teacher education. Drawing on data from interviews and learning analytics, findings reveal that gen-AI serves as both an emotional buffer and a cognitive scaffold. Pre-service teachers reported reduced emotional burden when gen-AI handled routine tasks, freeing cognitive resources for more complex pedagogical decision-making. However, over-reliance on gen-AI was associated with decreased self-efficacy in lesson planning. The study offers practical recommendations for integrating gen-AI in initial teacher education while maintaining the development of core teaching competencies.
