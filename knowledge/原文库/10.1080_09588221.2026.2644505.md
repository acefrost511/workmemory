## 摘要

This study investigates the use of data-driven learning (DDL) approaches to enhance learners' genre competence in proposal writing. Data-driven learning leverages corpus tools and concordance analysis to help language learners discover linguistic patterns independently. The study examines how learners develop genre awareness and writing competence through corpus-based inquiry and authentic data exposure. Using a qualitative research design, the study analyzes learner development in academic proposal writing genres, focusing on rhetorical moves, linguistic features, and discourse conventions. Findings indicate that DDL enhances learners' genre competence by making implicit linguistic patterns explicit and fostering learner autonomy in academic writing development.
