## 摘要

As AI-powered learning environments become more prevalent in higher education and workplace training, understanding adult learners' expectations and preferences for human-AI interaction in curriculum design is critical. This study aimed to determine adult learners' expectations regarding the design of AI-integrated curricula and their preferences for how AI should interact with learners during the learning process. Through a mixed-methods approach involving surveys and interviews with adult learners in professional development and continuing education programs, the researchers identified key themes: adult learners value AI that supports but does not replace human instruction, prefer transparency in AI decision-making, and want control over the level of AI intervention in their learning. The findings suggest that a well-structured AI-Augmented Design (AIAD) curriculum can facilitate both individual adult learning practices and lifelong learning when designed with adult learners' expectations in mind. The study provides design principles for creating AI-powered learning environments that respect adult learners' autonomy and professional identity.
