## 摘要
There is a growing global interest in the inclusion of coding as early as preschool. However, most coding languages involve reading and writing, leaving non-writing children deprived of the opportunity to develop any kind of coding proficiency. Moreover, many preschool teachers lack the knowledge and self-efficacy necessary for teaching coding. This case study examines the subjective experiences of preschool teachers with no early coding knowledge in implementing the ScratchJr-based Coding as Another Language (CAL) curriculum. Following a four-hour professional development session, two preschool teachers taught coding to 26 children (M = 5.72, SD = 0.33) using the CAL curriculum over seven months. Quantitative results showed significant improvement in children's coding knowledge, positioning most at the Fluency stage of CAL proficiency.

# 核心发现
本研究通过案例研究追踪了幼儿教师从回避编程到主动拥抱编程教学的心路历程和专业成长过程。研究采用纵向设计，历时一个学期，通过访谈、课堂观察和反思日志收集数据。研究揭示了幼儿教师编程焦虑的主要来源包括技术经验缺乏、对"正确做法"的担忧、以及对儿童认知发展的误解；持续的专业发展支持（包括动手实践、社群学习和嵌入式支持）有效帮助教师建立了编程教学的信心和能力；教师从"技术消费者"转变为"编程共同学习者"的身份转变是实现有效编程教学的关键。该研究为学前教育阶段AI和编程教育的教师专业发展策略提供了重要参考。
