## 摘要

The study examines how Slovenian students perceive the application of AI tools in Language for Specific Purposes (LSP) courses. It focuses on determining whether respondents' age, gender, and university enrolment influence their use of LSP course tools, and their perception of teachers' learning using AI tools. Conducted across four major universities, research involved 239 students who completed an anonymous online survey. Findings reveal that students frequently utilize tools such as ChatGPT, Grammarly, and Writer to prepare assignments and enhance writing quality. However, concerns about over-reliance, reduced critical thinking, and data accuracy persist. Gender differences were observed, with males more often using AI for peer communication assessment preparation. Teachers' attitudes varied across institutions, with some encouraging AI use and others warning about potential ethical risks. The study highlights the need for structured guidelines on AI integration in language curricula, enhanced teacher training, and promotion of balanced AI use alongside developing critical thinking skills.
