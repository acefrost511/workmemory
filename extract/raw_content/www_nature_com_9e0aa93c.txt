Abstract
--------

The burgeoning integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) technology within educational settings, particularly in personalized learning environments, has garnered significant interest. This paper delves into the perceptions and experiences of undergraduate students in Chinese universities concerning AI’s application in personalized learning. The study surveyed 48 undergraduates from a comprehensive Chinese university who had utilized generative AI, gathering their insights through semi-structured interviews. Thematic analysis was employed to organize and interpret the data. The findings underscore the positive impact of generative AI on students’ motivation and cognitive skills, alongside concerns regarding potential over-reliance leading to a decline in independent thinking. Student apprehensions include AI accuracy, data privacy, and algorithmic biases. The study advocates for a student-centric approach in AI research, stressing interdisciplinary collaboration to foster AI’s educational application. Additionally, it emphasizes the necessity of teacher training in AI integration to ensure effective pedagogical implementation. Ethical considerations and privacy safeguards in AI utilization are highlighted, signaling crucial areas for further inquiry and practical guidance.

Introduction
------------

In contemporary society, the rapid advancement of Artificial Intelligence (AI) is transforming various sectors, notably education. AI advancements are not merely technological feats; they fundamentally enhance teaching methodologies and learning experiences, providing robust support for the development of personalized learning environments (Crompton and Burke, [2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR9)). This evolution signifies a pivotal shift from basic automation to sophisticated decision-making support. Modern advancements in deep learning and machine learning have empowered AI systems to perform intricate cognitive tasks such as language processing, image recognition, and predictive analysis.

Within the educational sector, AI technologies are increasingly being integrated into smart teaching systems, learning analytics tools, and customized educational pathways, all aimed at boosting instructional efficacy and learning outcomes (Son et al., [2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR28)). A personalized learning environment, by definition, tailors the educational experience to meet individual learners’ specific needs and preferences. This approach not only supports learners in setting personal goals and managing their learning trajectory but also facilitates effective communication within the learning process, thereby optimizing resource allocation and fostering comprehensive development and lifelong learning through AI-driven solutions (Shemshack and Spector, [2020](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR26); Tetzlaff et al., [2021](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR29)).

The imperative to explore AI from the student perspective is grounded in the recognition that students are the primary beneficiaries of educational advancements. Their insights are critical in assessing the practical impact of AI technologies in educational settings (Wang et al., [2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR31)). Additionally, involving students in the design and implementation phases ensures that AI solutions are tailored to their actual learning needs rather than being driven solely by technological capabilities (Wang et al., [2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR31); Crompton and Burke, [2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR9)).

Research indicates varied student perceptions of AI in education. Many students appreciate AI’s role in providing timely formative feedback and supporting large-scale teaching operations through automated grading systems. They also respond positively to AI-driven personalization of learning materials, which adjusts content and difficulty based on individual performance metrics (Chan and Hu, [2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR6)). Despite some ethical reservations, the majority of students embrace AI applications within educational frameworks.

However, there remains a notable gap in understanding how generative AI specifically facilitates the development of personalized learning experiences and cognitive skills in educational settings. Cognitive skills refer to a student’s ability to process information, learn new concepts, and solve problems, encompassing memory, attention, comprehension, and reasoning abilities. Additionally, the impact of generative AI on student motivation and engagement, particularly regarding how these technologies can sustain or enhance students’ intrinsic and extrinsic motivation over time, is underexplored. Motivation includes both intrinsic factors, such as curiosity and personal satisfaction, and extrinsic factors, like grades and rewards, that drive students to engage in learning activities. Engagement describes the level of active participation and involvement in educational activities, reflected in students’ interactions, feedback, and interest in the learning process. Moreover, the challenges faced by both educators and students in the practical implementation and optimization of generative AI tools have not been sufficiently addressed. Addressing these gaps is crucial for advancing a more holistic and responsible application of AI in education, ensuring that AI technologies not only meet the personalized needs of learners but also adhere to ethical standards that protect student welfare and promote equitable learning opportunities (Crompton and Burke, [2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR9); Chiu, [2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR7); Romero and Ventura, [2007](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR24)). To address these gaps, three research questions are raised:

1. (1)
   
   How does generative AI facilitate the development of personalized learning experiences and cognitive skills in educational settings?
2. (2)
   
   What impact does generative AI have on enhancing student motivation and engagement in learning processes?
3. (3)
   
   How do educators and students address technical challenges and optimize the use of generative AI tools in educational contexts?

The significance of this research lies in its deep exploration of AI in education, particularly in personalized learning settings. By analyzing students’ experiences and perceptions, the study highlights AI’s potential to enhance teaching efficiency and learning outcomes, while also stressing the importance of student-centered AI research. Feedback from students is vital for gauging AI’s practical utility and ensuring that AI applications meet genuine learning needs. Therefore, the insights gained are crucial for blending AI with effective teaching methods and underscore the need for interdisciplinary research and teacher training in AI.

Literature review
-----------------

### Application and development of AI

AI technology has evolved significantly since its inception in the 1960s, beginning with simple chatbots like ELIZA and advancing to sophisticated learning algorithms. Developments in machine learning and deep learning have been particularly influential, enhancing AI’s capabilities across various domains (Labadze et al., [2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR19)). In education, AI has moved beyond basic automation to support complex teaching and learning activities. Today, AI-enhanced systems such as Intelligent Teaching Systems (ITS) and AI assistants offer personalized learning experiences, adapting to individual learner needs and providing immediate feedback. Examples of AI applications in education include the use of AI chatbots for homework assistance and interactive learning, as well as AI-driven assessment and prediction of student learning outcomes. These advancements not only enhance student learning but also save time for educators and improve pedagogical methods (Crompton and Burke, [2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR9); Labadze et al., [2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR19)).

### Construction of personalized learning environments and the role of AI

Personalized learning environments (PLEs) are designed to cater to students’ interests, needs, readiness, and motivation, making adaptive adjustments based on their progress to support lifelong learning. PLEs empower learners to organize their learning, select content, and facilitate communication and collaboration, extending learning beyond formal coursework (Bernacki et al., [2021](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR4)). AI, leveraging technologies such as machine learning and natural language processing, is pivotal in enhancing these environments. AI tailors teaching methods to individual learning preferences—whether visual, auditory, reading, writing, or hands-on practice (Huang et al., [2021](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR14)). Emerging AI-driven adaptive learning systems offer personalized course sequences, content recommendations, tasks, and automatic assessments, significantly improving the learning experience (Katiyar et al., [2024](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR17)). By analyzing educational big data, including learning histories, logs, and various student submissions, AI can optimize learning paths tailored to each student’s cognitive and behavioral patterns (Kim, [2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR18)). Furthermore, intelligent education systems utilize collaborative filtering algorithms based on knowledge graphs to effectively address issues such as “learning loss,” demonstrating the potential of personalized education systems to transform learning experiences” (Liu et al., [2021](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR21)).

### Application of AI technology from the student’s perspective

Students are pivotal beneficiaries in educational reform, particularly with the introduction of AI technologies like intelligent tutoring and automated assessment systems, which have notably improved STEM education quality (Xu and Ouyang, [2022](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR32)). Including students in the design and implementation of AI applications fosters more personalized and precise educational approaches. AI technologies are employed to analyze and predict academic achievements, offering personalized guidance based on students’ learning statuses and behaviors (Hwang et al., [2020](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR15)). Students generally appreciate AI-generated feedback, especially when it includes specific, clear, and corrective elements such as student code, which aligns with the standards of formative feedback (Zhang et al., [2024](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR33)). Furthermore, while students value feedback that provides ample code examples and maintains specificity and correctness, their opinions on the tone of the feedback vary (Zhang et al., [2024](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR33)). Research indicates that personalized learning significantly enhances student engagement, self-efficacy, and academic performance. Specifically, students’ attitudes towards technology-based chemistry learning have improved, showing a preference for personalized learning systems, which underscores the positive impact of tailored educational experiences (Aviran and Blonder, [2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR2)). However, the deployment of AI in education is not without challenges; it introduces ethical concerns, including data privacy, algorithm bias, transparency, and accountability. Both students and educators must be cognizant of these issues and engage responsibly with AI technologies (Akgun and Greenhow, [2022](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR1)).

### Students’ views on the impact of AI: cognitive skills, motivation, and engagement

In terms of cognitive skills, students generally believe that AI tools (such as intelligent tutoring systems and adaptive learning platforms) can significantly enhance their cognitive abilities. These tools help improve memory, attention, comprehension, and reasoning by providing personalized feedback and customized learning experiences. For example, a study found that students believe interacting with generative AI (GAI) tools can enhance academic performance by boosting self-efficacy and cognitive engagement (Lin et al., [2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR20)).

Motivation and engagement are key factors for the successful integration of AI in learning environments. Students report that AI technology can significantly increase their motivation and engagement by offering personalized and interactive learning experiences. For instance, research indicates that students believe AI chatbots can increase intrinsic motivation by meeting their needs for competence and relatedness (Silitonga et al., [2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR27)). Additionally, students feel that teacher support plays an important role in moderating the impact of AI on their motivation.

In personalized learning environments (PLEs), students believe that AI can analyze large amounts of data to create personalized learning paths, thereby improving learning efficiency and effectiveness. For example, studies have found that students think AI-supported personalized learning paths can adjust content and pace based on their performance and preferences, enhancing the learning experience (Bayly-Castaneda et al., [2024](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR3)). Furthermore, students have shown great interest in generative language models (such as ChatGPT), believing that these models can provide personalized feedback and support, further enhancing the learning experience (Jeon and Lee, [2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR16)).

Therefore, students generally believe that AI has great potential in improving cognitive skills, motivation, and engagement. These findings suggest that the application of AI technology in education can not only enhance learning outcomes but also create more engaging and motivating learning environments.

### Conceptual framework

Personalized learning environments (PLEs) have become a critical focus within educational technology research, emphasizing learner-centered approaches that facilitate personalized learning and effective resource management. This study’s conceptual framework draws on significant documents, including the “Australian Schools Generative AI Framework” ([2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR11)), the “Education 2030 Incheon Declaration” (2015), and UNESCO’s “Technical Note on National Commitments and Actions to Transform Education” (2022). These documents collectively provide a robust theoretical foundation for applying AI in educational settings, promoting systems that enable learners to set personalized goals, aggregate learning content, and manage their educational processes, ultimately fostering a lifelong virtual learning environment.

The exploration of AI’s impact on student learning is grounded in established educational psychology theories which inform our assessment of cognitive skills, motivation, and engagement. Cognitive skills, such as memory, attention, comprehension, and reasoning abilities, are measured through tools developed with guidance from Bloom’s Taxonomy. These assessments probe how AI influences students’ problem-solving capabilities and understanding of complex subjects. Motivation is explored through Ryan and Deci’s Self-Determination Theory, which helps us evaluate both intrinsic factors, like personal satisfaction and curiosity, and extrinsic factors, such as rewards and recognition, to determine how AI tools affect these motivational drivers.

Engagement is conceptualized as the degree of active participation in educational activities, essential for effective learning outcomes, and is assessed by monitoring the frequency and depth of students’ interactions with AI-enhanced learning tools (Zhang et al., [2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR34)). The interrelationship among these constructs—how students’ motivation influences their perceptions of AI applications, which in turn impacts their cognitive skills and engagement—plays a crucial role in enhancing learning outcomes. This comprehensive framework underpins the development of our interview guide and data analysis strategy, allowing for systematic collection and insightful analysis of students’ feedback on using AI in personalized learning environments.

This study not only explores the practical effectiveness of AI in education but also offers valuable insights for future educational technology innovations (Dabbagh, [2024](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR10)). By integrating these theoretical perspectives with empirical research, our approach ensures a thorough exploration of AI’s impact, enhancing the validity and applicability of our findings in understanding and optimizing AI’s role in education.

Methods
-------

### Participants and background

This study involves 48 Year 1 students from a higher education institution in Southeast China, recognized for its comprehensive educational structure and emphasis on technological advancement. The institution offers a wide range of disciplines, including economics, law, education, science, engineering, and the arts, within its 20 specialized colleges and honors colleges. The university’s focus on interdisciplinary education and international partnerships creates an ideal context for studying AI integration in higher education. The cohort in this study comprises 23 males and 25 females, aged 18 to 21, all of whom are majoring in disciplines such as engineering, computer science, mathematics, and economics. These students were enrolled in a selective course titled “AI in Academic Writing,” designed to enhance their academic writing skills through the use of AI tools. The course, worth 2 credits, was chosen for this study because it directly integrates AI into the curriculum, requiring students to engage with a designated AI platform for at least four hours weekly. This made the course an ideal setting for examining the practical use of AI in education. Institutional approval was secured before the study, ensuring all ethical standards were met. Students were selected through convenience sampling, focusing on those who had chosen to enroll in the “AI in Academic Writing” course. This approach allowed us to gather insights from participants who were already interested and engaged in using AI tools as part of their academic development.

### Research instruments

The research instruments for this study are meticulously designed to align with the conceptual framework centered around personalized learning environments (PLEs), supporting learners in setting goals, aggregating content, and managing their educational processes. This approach fosters a personalized and continuous learning experience.

The assessment tool used was a semi-structured interview structured around Bloom’s Taxonomy and Ryan and Deci’s Self-Determination Theory. For cognitive skills, questions such as, “Do you think generative AI can enhance your higher-order thinking abilities, such as creativity, critical thinking, and problem-solving abilities? If so, how does AI achieve the enhancement of these abilities?” assess students’ problem-solving skills and their ability to think critically. This approach directly relates to the cognitive skills aspect of our framework by exploring how generative AI influences these abilities.

For motivation, the instrument includes questions that measure both intrinsic and extrinsic motivational factors. For example, “Do you think generative AI can stimulate your learning motivation and interest? If so, how does generative AI stimulate your learning motivation and interest?” This question aims to assess students’ enthusiasm for learning tasks, their persistence in overcoming challenges, and the specific ways AI tools might foster their motivation.

Engagement is evaluated through questions that ask about the frequency and depth of AI tool usage, such as, “How do you think generative AI can enhance your learning engagement on online learning platforms (such as Personal Learning Environment PLE platforms)?” This helps assess the level of engagement and the perceived value of AI-enhanced interactions, which are crucial for effective learning outcomes.

To ensure that the instruments effectively capture the intended constructs, each question is grounded in both theoretical definitions and practical student responses. This alignment ensures that the data collected is robust and reliable, accurately reflecting the impact of AI on personalized learning.

### Data collection

Data collection was comprehensively carried out through structured interviews conducted within a Personalized Learning Environment (PLE) platform, Moodle, which was specifically customized to integrate AI tools for various academic tasks. This approach was designed to simulate real-world AI applications within educational settings effectively. To ensure students were well-prepared to use these AI tools, instructors provided extensive guidance and scaffolding, which included training sessions on effective interaction with AI and utilizing platform features to enhance learning outcomes, such as academic writing.

The interviews, lasting about 30 min each, were embedded seamlessly within the course activities to collect real-time, authentic feedback from students as they engaged with the AI tools. This method allowed for the immediate capture of relevant insights into their cognitive skills, motivation, and engagement, directly relating to their interactions with AI. The interviews were audio-recorded and transcribed verbatim using transcription software to maintain data integrity, and the content was then meticulously reviewed and edited for clarity and confidentiality.

Data from the interviews was systematically collected and exported from Moodle’s dedicated data collection module by the course instructors, ensuring completeness and accuracy. This structured approach ensured that the insights gathered were directly applicable to evaluating the theoretical constructs of cognitive skills, motivation, and engagement in an educational AI context.

### Data analysis

Data analysis in this study was rigorously performed using thematic analysis, a method well-established for its effectiveness in qualitative research. In line with the methodology described by Braun and Clarke ([2006](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR5)), the analysis process commenced with a thorough examination of the interview transcripts. The first author conducted an initial review, generating preliminary codes by identifying key phrases and sentences that directly corresponded to students’ interactions with AI tools, reflecting the constructs of cognitive skills, motivation, and engagement as defined by our conceptual framework.

These preliminary codes were then collaboratively discussed and categorized into potential themes by both the first and second authors. This collaborative phase was critical for maintaining a rigorous and unbiased coding process. Each researcher independently applied these codes, which provided a foundation for the initial thematic mapping. Following this, both researchers convened to compare their findings, refine the themes, and adjust the codes to ensure consistency and comprehensiveness in capturing the constructs of interest.

In a subsequent round of coding, adjustments were made to better reflect the data and ensure alignment with our conceptual framework and the relevant literature on educational psychology and AI’s impact on learning. This deductive approach allowed us to systematically categorize the data according to predefined concepts from our framework, such as cognitive skills and motivation.

The final stage of the analysis saw both researchers achieving an agreement of over 90% on the themes, codes, and quotations. This high level of concordance and reliability highlights the robustness of the analytical process. The results of this analysis are articulated with direct quotes from the interviews, vividly showcasing students’ perceptions and insights into how generative AI influences their learning experiences.

### Ethical consideration

During the implementation of this study, we adhered strictly to established ethical norms and standards. Before the research commencement, all participants provided informed consent, acknowledging their understanding of the research objectives, methodology, and their rights and responsibilities. We ensured that participants were aware of their right to withdraw from the study at any stage without any adverse consequences or pressure. Additionally, we guaranteed that all participant data would be managed securely, used solely for this study, and kept confidential. Throughout the research process, we respected and valued the students’ feedback without interfering with their opinions. We are grateful to all the students for their active participation, which was essential for the success of this research.

Results
-------

### Q1: How does generative AI promote the development of personalized learning and thinking skills in education and teaching?

Through the analysis of interview records and student feedback, four key themes were identified that capture the multifaceted impact of generative AI in education. These themes, detailed in Table [1](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#Tab1) with representative quotes, encompass the enhancement of personalized learning (Theme 1) and the development of thinking skills across various academic disciplines (see Table [1](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#Tab1)).

**Table 1 Themes identified in student interview records.**

[Full size table](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x/tables/1)

The analysis revealed that generative AI not only improves students’ learning efficiency but also fosters creativity, critical thinking, and problem-solving skills (Theme 6) through tailored learning paths and intelligent tutoring systems. Additionally, the use of AI tools in cognitive training, which includes providing instant feedback and highlighting common mistakes, supports students in self-reflection and the further development of their cognitive and thinking abilities (Theme 4). However, the study also highlights potential risks associated with AI usage, such as technological dependency and concerns over information accuracy, which require careful consideration and balanced implementation by educators (Theme 7).

In conducting the thematic analysis, the researchers systematically coded and categorized the interview records and student feedback. The process began with a thorough review of all data to identify initial codes. For example, when analyzing students’ use of generative AI in cognitive training, specific attention was given to instances where students mentioned receiving instant feedback or identifying and correcting mistakes. These insights were then organized into broader themes that reflect the various impacts of generative AI on education. Each theme was carefully defined, and representative quotes were included to ensure accuracy and relevance. For instance, in Theme 4, “Cognitive Training,” specific cases were identified where students used AI tools for self-reflection and cognitive development. These themes were validated through repeated discussions to ensure they were closely aligned with the research questions and accurately reflected the data.

To substantiate the findings on how generative AI enhances learning efficiency and develops cognitive skills, the study relied on qualitative data gathered from student interviews. Students provided detailed accounts of how AI tools helped them complete tasks more efficiently, offered new approaches to problem-solving, and encouraged creative thinking. For example, several students mentioned that AI tools provided immediate feedback that allowed them to quickly correct mistakes, thereby improving their efficiency in completing assignments. Others described how AI-generated suggestions inspired new ideas in their writing, indicating enhanced creativity. These narratives were instrumental in forming the themes and drawing conclusions about the positive impact of AI on their learning experiences.

The majority of students recognize the significant benefits of generative AI in enhancing learning efficiency across various academic contexts. For instance, 35 students reported that generative AI is particularly effective in assisting with homework and exam preparation by offering problem-solving strategies, refining articles, and creating course content. Additionally, 29 students highlighted the utility of AI tools in language learning and essay writing, noting that immediate feedback and suggestions for modifications substantially enhance writing quality and language proficiency. Moreover, 24 students found that AI tools are highly efficient in handling repetitive or time-sensitive tasks, effectively saving valuable learning time. One student mentioned, “When I am unable to accurately answer a question or precisely complete a task, I will use AI to assist with homework and exams, assist with essay writing, language learning, and various other aspects” (S1). Another student observed, “Generative AI can save me some time in searching for information, help me quickly and clearly locate the information I need, and generate relevant content based on my needs” (S12). Similarly, another noted, “I believe that generative AI can greatly assist my academics. By using these tools, I can obtain information and knowledge more quickly, and better understand and apply what I have learned” (S15).

Students generally recognize generative AI as a valuable tool for enhancing their thinking skills. Specifically, 25 students reported using AI to generate diverse perspectives, aiding them in understanding problems from various angles. Additionally, 21 students highlighted AI’s ability to pose novel questions, sparking their curiosity and eagerness to explore further. Meanwhile, 18 students noted that AI helps them devise innovative solutions, thereby enhancing their problem-solving capabilities. One student commented, “AI can train my thinking by generating different perspectives, proposing new questions, and creating new solutions” (S11).

According to the interview records, most students acknowledge the positive impact of generative AI on enhancing higher-order thinking skills. 33 students noted that AI fosters creativity by offering diverse perspectives and innovative solutions. Additionally, 30 students reported that interacting with AI has helped them learn to pose more critical questions and analyze different viewpoints, thus improving their critical thinking skills. 28 students commented on AI’s usefulness in simulating complex scenarios and providing problem-solving strategies, which enhances their problem-solving abilities. For instance, one student shared, “Generative AI helps to enhance higher-order thinking skills. It stimulates creative thinking, encourages critical thinking, and solves problems” (S48). Another student echoed this sentiment, stating, “AI can help me get information faster and provide credible data and views from various perspectives. It may generate novel viewpoints, insights, or problem-solving methods, thereby stimulating innovative and inspiring thinking” (S4).

Despite the advantages, several challenges were identified. 15 students reported that AI tools sometimes failed to fully comprehend their queries, resulting in responses that fell short of their expectations. Additionally, 10 students encountered difficulties in obtaining accurate answers or opinions from AI tools on highly subjective questions, highlighting areas where AI technology may need further development and fine-tuning. As one student pointed out, “The help is very huge, it can fundamentally participate in the progress of my studies. However, there are difficulties in guiding keywords and correcting the direction of questions. AI’s understanding of sentences cannot be satisfactory, and the correction process may even increase its misunderstanding” (S36).

While AI tools offer numerous benefits, some students have also expressed concerns about potential risks associated with their use. 22 students worry that excessive reliance on AI could diminish their ability to think independently. 15 students raised concerns that inaccuracies or biases in the information provided by AI could mislead their thinking and decision-making processes. Additionally, 9 students highlighted privacy and ethical issues, voicing apprehensions about the potential risks to personal privacy and data security associated with using AI tools. One student articulated this concern by stating, “Over-reliance on AI tools may lead to a loss of independent thinking ability, over-dependence on AI-generated viewpoints and solutions, and a decrease in autonomy and critical thinking” (S12).

### Q2: What is the effect of generative AI in enhancing student motivation and engagement?

The analysis of student interviews identified three key themes demonstrating how generative AI enhances student motivation and engagement in learning, particularly in online environments where direct interaction is minimal. These themes reflect the significant value of generative AI in educational technology.

The first theme highlights the role of personalized recommendations and intelligent tutoring features of generative AI, which significantly boost students’ enthusiasm. Specifically, 42 students noted that AI provides customized learning resources and suggestions tailored to their learning behavior, interests, and historical performance, making learning more targeted and effective. Additionally, 36 students emphasized the benefits of real-time feedback and intelligent tutoring systems, which enhance interactivity and participation in learning. As student S12 stated, “Generative AI can help us generate personalized learning content based on our interests and learning styles, making learning more targeted and interesting.”

The second theme focuses on the interactivity and authenticity of AI-generated content. Students provided positive feedback on these aspects, noting that personalized learning materials generated by AI were not only interesting but also easy to understand, thereby increasing their engagement. Student S3 remarked, “Generative AI can provide interesting and attractive learning materials, helping us better understand and master knowledge.” This personalized approach was particularly valuable in enhancing students’ motivation and sustaining their enthusiasm in non-traditional educational settings.

The third theme reveals that AI tools are crucial in sparking students’ interest and providing a personalized learning experience, which is essential for sustaining enthusiasm, especially in remote education environments. Specifically, 34 students reported that AI meets their unique learning needs by offering personalized content and interactive experiences, significantly enhancing their interest. Additionally, 22 students valued AI’s ability to offer timely feedback and pose challenging questions, which helped sustain motivation and engagement. For example, student S5 mentioned, “When I encounter difficulties, AI can use its knowledge base to provide suggestions, helping me complete some seemingly meaningless tasks, allowing me to invest more time and energy into learning.”

However, several challenges were also identified. 12 students expressed concerns that excessive reliance on AI might undermine their autonomous learning capabilities. Furthermore, 9 students raised issues about potential biases and privacy risks associated with AI recommendation systems. Some students expressed reservations about AI’s effectiveness in stimulating learning motivation and interest, noting that while AI offers valuable information and resources, the primary drivers of learning motivation are still personal goals and interests. As student S17 observed, “AI sometimes proposes terms and viewpoints that I am not familiar with. I will analyze them and also go online to read books and search; it can better help me understand my own discipline and also motivate me to learn.”

Additionally, students reported various feelings encountered when using generative AI, ranging from surprise and curiosity to inspiration and cognitive expansion. Specifically, 34 students expressed surprise at the content generated by AI, as it provided new perspectives and solutions they had not considered before. For instance, student S1 shared, “We may encounter some content generated by AI that surprises, amazes, or inspires us. For example, AI that generates realistic dialogs through natural language processing makes us feel like we are communicating with real people.” Similarly, student S15 noted, “Because of AI’s huge and comprehensive knowledge reserve, the content generated by AI often surprises, intrigues, or inspires me. These situations stimulate my curiosity and prompt me to explore and learn related topics more deeply.”

In summary, while generative AI significantly enhances student motivation and engagement through personalized learning experiences, challenges such as potential over-reliance and issues related to biases and privacy must be addressed to ensure effective integration into educational settings.

### Q3: When using generative AI tools, how do educators and students overcome technical challenges and maximize their potential in education?

The analysis of student interviews identified three key themes that capture both the technical challenges and educational opportunities associated with generative AI tools. These themes include the technical proficiency required to use AI tools, concerns over content accuracy, and the broader implications of AI in education.

Students generally acknowledge that utilizing generative AI tools demands a certain level of technical knowledge, often raising concerns regarding the accuracy of the content generated. 16 students highlighted that beginners might face technical barriers, such as learning how to interact effectively with AI and setting appropriate parameters to achieve optimal results. Additionally, 8 students mentioned the need to invest extra time and effort to learn and adapt to the operation of AI tools. Student S36 noted, “When using generative AI, there are difficulties in guiding keywords and correcting the direction of questions. AI’s understanding of sentences cannot be satisfactory, and the correction process may even increase misunderstandings.”

Accuracy remains a significant concern. 21 students reported that the content generated by AI tools could sometimes be erroneous or inaccurate, necessitating user verification and filtering. This potential for inaccuracy is particularly concerning as it could impact decision-making and judgment in critical areas such as academic research or important tasks. Student S32 mentioned, “Generative AI can assist my studies to a certain extent, especially in providing a large amount of text content. However, I have encountered some difficulties in using these tools, such as the generated content may not be accurate or coherent enough, requiring me to spend extra time for correction and modification.”

Despite these challenges, students recognize the vast educational opportunities that the widespread adoption of generative AI might offer, such as personalized learning, enhanced teaching efficiency, and the development of innovative teaching methods. 22 students noted that generative AI significantly enhances their learning efficiency by providing personalized resources, assisting in information search and organization, and facilitating creative thinking. Student S24 summarized the mixed experience, stating, “AI provides me with several directions and relevant materials, which greatly shortens my creation time and allows more time for learning professional knowledge. However, sometimes the content generated by AI does not match what I want, making it cumbersome to ask questions.”

However, students also highlighted significant challenges, such as ensuring data security, protecting privacy, and verifying the authenticity of information. 25 students expressed concerns about data security and privacy, especially given the large volumes of personal data that AI systems often handle. For instance, student S34 pointed out, “The current stage of AI still has problems with the authenticity of information. For some professional questions, AI will not directly say that it does not understand, but will provide wrong answers, causing self-misleading.” Additionally, student S35 remarked, “Generative AI will become a major weapon in attacking information security in the future. When changing a perspective to collect money, AI will also become a shield to guard information security.”

Students believe that the widespread adoption of generative AI will unlock significant opportunities in the education sector, including personalized learning, enhanced teaching efficiency, and the introduction of innovative teaching methods. 23 students emphasized that AI can tailor learning content and methods to individual students’ interests, abilities, and learning styles, thereby improving learning outcomes. 18 students noted that AI’s ability to automatically grade assignments and assess student performance can significantly reduce the workload for teachers, enhancing teaching efficiency. Additionally, 15 students highlighted that AI technology facilitates educational innovation, such as incorporating virtual reality and augmented reality, providing a more immersive and enriched learning experience. As student S30 stated, “The popularization of generative AI in the future will bring many opportunities to the education sector, such as personalized learning, enhancing students’ learning experience, and improving the quality of education.”

In sum, while students recognize the benefits of generative AI, including personalized learning and improved teaching efficiency, they also acknowledge the challenges it brings, particularly regarding data security, privacy protection, and content authenticity. These findings underscore the necessity for educators and students to address technical challenges proactively and harness the full potential of generative AI in educational settings.

Discussion
----------

The results from this study underscore the significant capabilities of generative AI in enhancing educational outcomes, particularly in fostering personalized learning and critical thinking skills. Students reported improvements in learning efficiency and a notable enhancement in their creativity, critical thinking, and problem-solving abilities through the use of generative AI tools. These findings are consistent with the observations of Mao, Chen, and Liu ([2024](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR22)), who noted that AI can spur creativity by providing diverse perspectives and innovative solutions.

Generative AI not only enhances learning efficiency but also supports students in self-reflection through tailored learning paths and intelligent tutoring systems. Thematic analysis revealed that AI tools are instrumental in providing instant feedback and highlighting common mistakes, which contribute to cognitive and thinking skill development (Theme 4). This aligns with the work of Seo et al. ([2021](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR25)), who demonstrated that AI systems enhance online education through personalized support, automation of routine tasks, and adaptive assessments. Additionally, students recognized AI’s capacity to generate diverse perspectives and pose novel questions, thereby stimulating curiosity, problem-solving skills, and creative thinking.

However, integrating generative AI into education presents several challenges. Students reported mixed experiences with AI tools; while these tools were beneficial for tasks such as assignment assistance, exam preparation, and language learning, they sometimes fell short in addressing complex or subjective inquiries. This variability in effectiveness aligns with observations by Ghimire et al. ([2024](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR12)), who highlighted that the performance of AI language models can be inconsistent depending on the complexity of the tasks. Moreover, the integration of AI into educational assessments raises concerns regarding fairness and accuracy, as also discussed by Mao et al. ([2024](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR22)).

Technical hurdles persist, as students indicated that a substantial level of technical knowledge is necessary to fully leverage AI capabilities. Some students reported difficulties in interacting effectively with AI, especially in setting appropriate parameters to achieve optimal results. The findings suggest that students may circumvent traditional learning and assessment processes when utilizing AI tools, potentially compromising educational outcomes (Cornell University, [2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR8)). Holmes and Miao ([2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR13)) emphasize the importance of comprehensive guidelines to ensure the responsible utilization of AI technologies in educational settings.

The study also highlights concerns regarding the over-reliance on AI, which could negatively impact students’ independent learning abilities. Although AI-generated personalized recommendations and intelligent tutoring systems significantly enhance online learning experiences, excessive reliance may undermine autonomy and critical thinking. This concern is supported by Vasconcelos et al. ([2023](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR30)), who noted the tendency of individuals to accept AI-generated recommendations even when incorrect, and Passi and Vorvoreanu ([2022](/articles/s41599-024-04168-x#ref-CR23)), who discussed the challenges users face in evaluating their reliance on AI systems.

Students also expressed concerns about potential risks, such as privacy, data security, and the authenticity of information generated by AI. They emphasized the need for a balanced implementation of AI that maintains educational integrity while maximizing the benefits of personalization and efficiency. Overall, while generative AI presents tremendous potential for enhancing educational outcomes and fostering higher-order thinking skills, its deployment must be carefully managed to mitigate the risks of technological dependency and ensure information accuracy.

In sum, this study provides empirical evidence on the dual nature of generative AI in education: on one hand, it effectively enhances learning efficiency and cognitive skills, while on the other, it introduces challenges related to reliance, technical barriers, and ethical concerns. These findings call for a balanced and informed approach to integrating AI into educational environments, emphasizing the importance of proper guidelines, technical support, and the responsible use of AI tools to maximize their educational benefits.

Conclusion
----------

This study underscores both the transformative potential and inherent challenges associated with the integration of generative AI in educational contexts. Through a qualitative examination involving 48 undergraduate students, the findings reveal that generative AI significantly enhances personalized learning and the development of cognitive skills, including creativity, critical thinking, and problem-solving. These outcomes illustrate the substantial benefits of AI technologies in providing individualized learning pathways, tailored instructional resources, and intelligent tutoring, thereby fostering increased student engagement and motivation.

However, the study also identifies significant challenges that must be addressed to ensure responsible and effective AI integration in education. While generative AI offers considerable advantages, the risk of technological over-reliance presents potential drawbacks, such as undermining students’ capacity for independent thinking. This underscores the necessity for comprehensive guidelines addressing data privacy, algorithm transparency, and ethical AI use, which are essential for safeguarding both students and educators and promoting equitable and effective personalized learning opportunities. Educators must play a pivotal role in the integration of AI technologies, positioning them as supportive tools that complement, rather than replace, traditional teaching practices.

For policymakers, the findings highlight the importance of establishing robust policies to regulate data privacy, ensure the accuracy and transparency of AI algorithms, and address ethical concerns surrounding AI implementation. Additionally, the study emphasizes the critical need for interdisciplinary collaboration and ongoing professional development for educators, enabling them to effectively integrate AI technologies into their pedagogical approaches. Promoting students’ understanding of AI, alongside fostering their critical thinking skills, is crucial to maximizing the potential benefits of AI while mitigating associated risks.

For researchers, this study contributes valuable empirical insights into the application of AI in educational settings, highlighting the need for further exploration of its long-term effects and cross-cultural applicability. Future research should consider larger and more diverse sample populations to enhance the generalizability of findings and employ quantitative methods alongside qualitative approaches to validate observed trends comprehensively. Including educators’ perspectives in future research would also provide a more nuanced understanding of the role of AI in teaching and learning, given their integral role in effectively deploying AI technologies in the classroom.

The core contribution of this study lies in presenting a balanced perspective on the dual-edged nature of AI in education, providing actionable insights into effectively harnessing the technology while maintaining ethical integrity and educational efficacy. The study acknowledges several limitations, including the limited sample size, which restricts the generalizability of the findings, and the exclusive reliance on qualitative data, which may introduce subjective biases. Additionally, the study does not address the long-term effects of AI usage in education, emphasizing the need for longitudinal research to examine the sustained impact of AI on student learning outcomes, cognitive development, and motivation.

In conclusion, generative AI holds significant promise as a transformative tool in education, with the potential to enrich personalized learning and enhance cognitive skill development. Nevertheless, its integration must be approached with careful consideration of ethical, technical, and pedagogical dimensions to ensure that it effectively supports student learning and development rather than impeding it. Further exploration in these areas is essential to provide stronger evidence and deeper insights that will guide the responsible and impactful integration of AI in educational environments.



Data availability
-----------------

The data supporting this study, provided as supplementary files to the manuscript, involves 48 students from a higher education institution in China. All these students have engaged with generative Artificial Intelligence (AI) for learning purposes. The cohort includes 23 males and 25 females, aged between 18 and 21. Each student has committed to using a designated AI platform for a minimum of four hours weekly. Institutional approval was obtained prior to the commencement of the study. Data collection was carried out through a survey of 10 questions distributed via the Personal Learning Environment (PLE) platform, where students provided real-time feedback directly on the forum. This approach facilitated immediate interaction and data capture, providing the research with timely and contextually relevant insights. The data acquisition address is as follows: <https://doi.org/10.6084/m9.figshare.25824532.v1>.

### Ethical approval

This study adhered to the ethical standards set by the Stamford Institutional Review Board (SIU2023324), with approval granted on 03/24/2023. All research activities complied with applicable guidelines and regulations for studies involving human participants. The approved project, titled “Exploring the Impact of Artificial Intelligence Application in Personalized Learning Environments: Thematic Analysis of Undergraduates’ Perception in China,” covered the research period from 03/25/2023 to 07/24/2023. The principal investigator, Xibing Wang, and co-investigators successfully completed CITI Training, with all certifications finalized on 02/02/2023.

### Informed consent

This study strictly adhered to ethical standards, ensuring that informed consent was obtained from all participants. Comprehensive written consent forms were provided, clearly outlining the research objectives, participants’ rights, obligations, and the scope of their involvement. These forms were personally signed by the participants on March 24, 2023. Consent was collected by Xibing Wang & Xiaoshu Xu from 48 first-year students at Wenzhou University. The consent covered participation, data usage, and publication, ensuring participants’ rights were fully protected. All documentation has been meticulously managed and is available upon request for peer review or post-publication inquiries. Participants were assured anonymity and were fully informed about the study’s purpose, how their data would be used, and any potential risks involved. This ethical commitment underscores our dedication to safeguarding participants’ welfare and maintaining research integrity.
