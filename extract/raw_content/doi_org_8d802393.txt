## Abstract
Computational Thinking (CT) capabilities are crucial for students’ future development. As a pivotal mode of thought, CT extends beyond mere programming skills, representing a methodology and strategy for problem-solving that empowers students to address complex challenges across diverse domains. In this context, this study aims to investigate the impact of a teaching strategy combining Project-Based Learning with Pair Programming Instructional Strategy (PBL-PPIS) on high school students’ CT capabilities and metacognitive skills. Conducted in a public high school in H City, Central China, this quasi-experimental design spanned one academic term and involved 90 first-year high school students aged 14 to 16. These students were divided into an experimental group and a control group, with the former utilizing the PBL-PPIS strategy and the latter adhering to conventional Project-Based Learning methods (PBL). To comprehensively assess the impact, this study utilized specialized scales for Computational Thinking and metacognitive abilities, and employed detailed analyses through paired sample t-tests and univariate ANCOVA. Through pre- and post-experiment surveys, we analyzed and compared the performance differences in CT and metacognitive skills between the two groups. The findings indicate significant enhancements in the experimental group across the five core competencies of Computational Thinking (Creativity, Algorithmic Thinking, Cooperativity, Critical Thinking, Problem Solving) and in their metacognitive abilities (planning, monitoring, evaluating). These results validate the effectiveness of the PBL-PPIS strategy in integrating the advantages of project-based learning and pair programming, underscoring its significant role in enhancing students’ CT and metacognitive abilities. This study contributes novel insights to the field of educational practice, offering fresh inspiration and direction for educators in designing and implementing programming education strategies.

### Ethical approval
Given the educational nature of this research, which focused on evaluating the impact of the Project-Based Learning and Pair Programming Integrated Strategy (PBL-PPIS) on high school students’ Computational Thinking and metacognitive skills, ethical considerations were meticulously addressed. This study involved students from a public high school in H City, Central China, and did not include the collection of sensitive personal data or expose participants to any physical or psychological risks.In alignment with educational research standards and in compliance with relevant data protection regulations, all necessary precautions were taken to safeguard the privacy and confidentiality of the participating students. The study was designed to ensure the anonymity of student data, with all identifying information being removed or obscured in the process of data collection and analysis. Parental consent was obtained for all participating minors, and the students were informed about the study’s objectives, their voluntary participation, and their right to withdraw at any time without consequences.In light of these considerations, and given the non-invasive and low-risk nature of the study, formal ethical approval from an institutional review board was deemed unnecessary. However, all procedures performed in the study were in accordance with the ethical standards of the institutional research committee and with the 1964 Helsinki declaration and its later amendments or comparable ethical standards.This approach ensured the ethical integrity of the research while respecting the rights and well-being of all participants, thereby upholding the highest standards of academic research ethics in the field of educational practice.

### Informed consent
All participants provided informed consent, demonstrating their understanding and voluntary agreement to participate in this study.
### Research involving human and animals participants
This research involved human participants, specifically focusing on first-year high school students in H City, Central China. The study’s objective was to investigate the impact of the Project-Based Learning and Pair Programming Integrated Strategy (PBL-PPIS) on students’ Computational Thinking (CT) capabilities and metacognitive skills. In executing this quasi-experimental research, which included gathering data through specialized scales and surveys, all necessary precautions were taken to ensure the ethical treatment of the human participants involved.The study adhered to stringent ethical standards in its approach to research involving human participants. Prior to the commencement of the study, informed consent was obtained from the participants and, where applicable, from their legal guardians, given the age range of the students (14 to 16 years). The consent process included a comprehensive explanation of the study’s purpose, methodology, and the voluntary nature of participation, with assurances that students could withdraw at any stage without any adverse consequences.To protect the privacy and confidentiality of the participants’ personal information, data anonymization procedures were rigorously implemented. This ensured that all collected data were processed in a manner that maintained the anonymity of the students involved. The research team was committed to upholding the highest standards of privacy and confidentiality in accordance with applicable privacy protection laws. We, as the authors of this manuscript, acknowledge our ethical and legal responsibilities to the human participants in this research. We affirm that the study was conducted in full compliance with ethical standards and legal regulations pertinent to educational research involving minors. This commitment underpins the integrity and credibility of our research findings and contributes to the ethical advancement of educational practices in the digital era.
