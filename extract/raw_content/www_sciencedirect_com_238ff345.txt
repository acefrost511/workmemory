## Abstract
Automated essay scoring, one of the crucial educational applications in the [natural language processing](/topics/computer-science/natural-language-processing) community, seeks to score the quality of essays through computer technology automatically, which has garnered increasing attention due to its diverse range of research challenges and potential utility. In this paper, we provide a brief introduction to the recent advances in automated essay scoring tasks, including detailed datasets and [evaluation metrics](/topics/computer-science/evaluation-metric), as well as traditional and neural approaches, and discuss future trends. This survey aims to provide researchers at the intersection of [artificial intelligence](/topics/computer-science/artificial-intelligence) and educational assessment with the most up-to-date information on automated essay scoring research, as well as fundamental support for researchers just entering the field, and also provide cutting-edge ideas for industrial applications.
## Introduction
Writing is an important means for the linguistic expression and utilization as well as the cognitive thinking ability of students to be reflected, and how to evaluate the writing level is a critical topic in educational measurement [1]. Exploring the in-depth integration of artificial intelligence (AI) and examination measurement has also become an increasingly hot issue in recent years [2], [3]. Manual evaluation of writing has drawbacks such as a high workload, resource consumption, strong subjectivity, poor consistency, etc., but automatic evaluation may swiftly and simply produce objective and fair results, which has a high application potential. With the rapid development of artificial intelligence, the direct use of computer programs to achieve automatic evaluation of essays has gradually matured, while the advancement of research to assist in the improvement of the writing abilities of students has also become a possibility.
Automatic essay scoring (AES) was initially introduced in the project essay grader (PEG) system established by Page in 1966 [4], with the purpose of automatically analyzing the quality of essays based on given prompts and scoring them through computer technology [5]. AES involves the disciplines of computer science, linguistics, statistics, educational psychology, cognitive science, etc., and current models mainly utilize AI technologies like natural language processing (NLP) and computer vision (CV).
Along with the rapid development of the AES field over the last roughly 60 years, AES methods based on NLP techniques have undergone quick paradigm shifts and iterative upgrades. The progress of AES methods and tasks with NLP paradigms is shown in Fig. 1. At the beginning, AES focuses on holistic scoring (the quality of an article is summarized by a score) [4], [6], which is consistent with the approach of expert systems and knowledge bases in the mainstream of NLP, in which essays are represented by experts constructing handcrafted features and scores are obtained using linear regression. Between 1990 and 2010, more NLP tasks were based on statistical machine learning, and the studies of AES also attempted to refine machine learning algorithms to improve the scoring results while enhancing trait-independent manual features. At the same time, various AES systems such as E-rater [7], [8],1 IntelliMetric [9],2 Intelligence Essay Assessor [10], etc. emerged. Furthermore, holistic scoring is no longer sufficient to meet application requirements, and students find it difficult to improve their writing skills based on their scores. Thus, more and more studies are aiming to mine trait features of specific dimensions for scoring, such as coherence [11], organization [12], clarity [13], prompt relevance [14], argument strength [15], etc. In 2016, data-driven neural-based methods were introduced [16]. To address issues such as insufficient training data due to the difficulty of labeling data, more researchers have concentrated on cross-domain research, in which model training with labeled data for a specific prompt is tested against data from an unseen prompt. The emergence of pre-trained language models (PLMs) and large language models (LLMs) has also allowed AES to be explored in many new directions [17], [18], [19].
Our motivation stems from the intricacy of the AES task and the still-growing necessity for online educational quality evaluation in the post-COVID pandemic era. We expect that this survey can provide researchers at the intersection of AI and educational assessment with the most up-to-date information on AES research, as well as fundamental support for researchers just entering the field, and also provide cutting-edge ideas for industrial applications.
This paper provides a brief overview of recent advances in AES. Section 2 introduces the fundamentals, including datasets, evaluation metrics, and a categorization of AES at different levels. In Section 3, we summarize traditional AES systems. We discuss in detail the neural AES approaches from the dimensions of model, strategy, optimization, etc. in Section 4. We describe the research trends and challenges of current hotspots in Section 5. Finally, in Section 6, we summarize the conclusions of this paper and directions for future work.

## Present: neural network methods of AES
Over the past decade, the research paradigm for AES has steadily shifted from statistical machine learning based on manual features to deep learning based on neural networks that automatically model essay features, which saves time and labor compared to manual feature methods. Presently, AES based on neural network approaches dominates the field and has consistently demonstrated improved performance.
