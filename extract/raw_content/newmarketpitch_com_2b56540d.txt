Key funding trends in the AI in education market
------------------------------------------------

Here is an updated table that ranks the top startups in the AI in education market based on the total amount of funding they have raised to date.

**Insights**

* MagicSchool AI grew from a $2.4M seed in August 2023 to $62M total by January 2025, completing three rounds in just 17 months, making it one of the fastest funding trajectories in the AI education market.
* AMBOSS raised $260M in a single growth round, representing nearly 87% of its $298M total, after operating for over seven years on just $38M. Capital efficiency in AI education can attract outsized late-stage conviction.
* Language learning dominates the top of the AI education funding table. Preply, Speak, ELSA, Praktika, Univerbal, and Blue Canoe collectively raised over $400M, making language learning the single largest sub-vertical by capital deployed in the AI education market.
* Teacher-focused AI tools (MagicSchool AI, Brisk Teaching, Curipod, Teacher's Buddy, Chalkie) collectively raised over $90M, signaling that investor appetite in the AI education space has expanded well beyond student-facing tutoring products.
* Oboe raised $20M across two AI education rounds in just two months (October to December 2025), moving from seed to an a16z-led Series A at an unusually compressed pace.
* Vimi, Giant, and Pensive each raised $7M to $12M seed rounds in early 2026, suggesting seed-stage valuations in AI education have reset sharply upward compared to even 18 months prior.
* Goodwater Capital backed Teachy, RevisionDojo, SuperKalam, and Ello across four different continents, suggesting a deliberate global AI in education portfolio strategy from one of the more active specialist investors in this dataset.

A few words about our methodology
---------------------------------

As you can see, we built a database that ranks startups in the AI in education market based on their total cumulative fundraising. To create this ranking, we reviewed many sources and cross-checked information across multiple places.

Whenever possible, we prioritized official company communications, since they are the most reliable source for funding amounts. When those were not available, we relied on reputable industry sources such as TechCrunch, Crunchbase, Financial Times or Forbes (to name a few).

We excluded random blogs, unverified websites, and any sources that could not be validated.

When funding rounds were announced in other currencies such as euros, Swiss francs, Singapore dollars, Australian dollars, or rupees, we converted them into approximate USD equivalents for consistency.

Sometimes different sources report slightly different numbers, or the exact round size is not fully disclosed. In those cases, we flag the uncertainty and assign a confidence label to each startup, visible in the last column.

Here is what they mean.

**Full confidence**: The company's equity fundraising history can be reconstructed completely from public sources. The rounds, dates, amounts, and key investors are clearly identified, with no meaningful gaps.

**Strong confidence**: The fundraising history is largely complete and reliable. There may be a small missing detail, such as incomplete investor information or a minor round with limited data, but the overall record is clear.

**Partial confidence**: The main fundraising rounds can be identified, but the record is incomplete or somewhat mixed. Some rounds may be missing or certain funding events may be difficult to separate clearly.

**Low confidence**: Public information is too limited, inconsistent, or ambiguous to reliably reconstruct the company's equity fundraising history.

When the confidence level is too low, we take a conservative approach and exclude the company from the ranking. We don't want to include data that cannot be reliably verified.

This reflects how we conduct all our research, including the work behind **[our report covering the AI in education market](https://newmarketpitch.com/pages/ai-education)**.

In a world where LLMs hallucinate and unreliable information is everywhere, our goal is simple: provide data you can trust.
