Special Issue: Generative AI and the Future of K-12 Education - Towards Sustainable and Ethical Innovations to Strengthen Human Agency
======================================================================================================================================


Participating journal: [Educational technology research and development](/journal/11423)







Open for submissions



Submission deadline30 May 2026


***The submission deadline for proposals has passed and editorial decisions have been communicated to authors. Authors with accepted proposals are invited to complete full paper submission via ETRD's online system, Editorial Manager, by May 30, 2026.***

This special issue of *Educational Technology Research and Development* aims **to advance research, theory, and practice on the responsible, sustainable, and ethical use of GenAI in K-12 education, promoting the significance of human agency within GenAI implementations**. We invite contributions that explore and discuss the ethical equity and policy dimensions of GenAI adoption in K-12 educational contexts. Submissions may include **theoretical reviews discussing frameworks, theoretical and conceptual analyses, ethical implications, empirical studies, and critical reviews**. Collectively, these contributions will help shape a research agenda that supports educators, researchers, and policymakers in leveraging GenAI ethically and effectively to advance K–12 teaching and learning. The topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

• **Concepts and Frameworks**: Systematic reviews and meta-analyses examining the sustainable and ethical use of GenAI in K-12 education.

• **Ethical Implications:** Analyses of the ethical dimensions and broader implications of GenAI use in K-12 contexts, with particular attention to equity, inclusion, and access – ensuring that AI benefits diverse learners while mitigating bias and inequality.

• **Empirical studies**: Quantitative, qualitative, and mixed methods research exploring AI ethics and human agency in K-12 educational settings.

• **Design implications and solutions**: Human-centred and ethically informed design of AI-powered tools and technology-enhanced learning settings.

• **Curriculum Integration and Student Agency:** Strategies for promoting the ethical and responsible use of GenAI in K-12 curricula across subjects, fostering student creativity, agency, and critical engagement with AI tools.

**Submission Guidelines**

Authors should submit a proposal, in the form of a 500–750 word abstract, a tentative title, author(s) information, keywords, and references along with an outline by **January 31, 2026**. Early submissions are encouraged.

  ### Panagiotis Kosmas
  
  Corresponding Guest Editor, Department of Education, University of Limassol, Cyprus, [panagiotis.kosmas@uol.ac.cy](mailto:panagiotis.kosmas@uol.ac.cy)
  
  Panagiotis Kosmas is an Assistant Professor of Educational Technology, Chair of the Department of Education and Director of the Master’s program in “Special Education and New Technologies”. He holds a PhD in Educational Technology, an MEd in Pedagogical Sciences from the Università Degli Studi Roma, an MA in New Technologies for Learning and Communication, and a BA in Classical Studies from the National University of Athens. His research expertise lies in integrating emerging technologies into formal educational settings, including K-12 education, Secondary Education, and higher education contexts.

  ### Maria Perifanou
  
  University of Macedonia, SMILE Lab, Greece, [maria.perifanou@uom.edu.gr](mailto:maria.perifanou@uom.edu.gr)
  
  Maria Perifanou is a senior researcher at the Smart and Mobile Interactive Learning Environments (SMILE) Lab at the University of Macedonia, Greece, and an adjunct professor in Technology Enhanced Learning (TEL) and Computer-Assisted Language Learning (CALL) at several Greek universities. Her research encompasses intelligent language learning technologies, MOOCs, OERs, AI in education, and educational robotics. She serves as Vice President of the European Association of Technology-Enhanced Learning (EATEL), General Chair of its JTEL Summer School, and is a board member of IAALDE.

  ### Michail Giannakos
  
  Department of Computer Science, Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU), Norway, [michailg@ntnu.no](mailto:michailg@ntnu.no)
  
  Michail Giannakos is a professor of interaction design and learning technologies at the Department of Computer Science of NTNU, and Head of the Learner-Computer Interaction lab. He is also a co-director of the AI Centre for Empowerment of Human Learning (AI-LEARN) — one of six national AI centres in Norway. His work focuses on developing new ways for humans to interact with intelligent learning systems and co-exist with intelligent learning systems. He has served in several commissions of trust for the European Commission (EC), the US-NSF and several other national and international agencies. He is the editor-in-chief of the Behaviour & Information Technology journal. His research has been funded by diverse sources like the EC, Microsoft Research, Meta Reality Labs, the Research Council of Norway (RCN), US-NSF, and the Cheng Endowment; Giannakos is also a recipient of a Marie Curie/ERCIM fellowship and the prestigious «Young Research Talents» grant from the RCN.

  ### Roger Azevedo
  
  University of Central Florida, School of Modeling, Simulation, and Training, USA, [roger.azevedo@ucf.edu](mailto:roger.azevedo@ucf.edu)
  
  Roger Azevedo is a Professor at the School of Modeling, Simulation, and Training at the University of Central Florida. His interdisciplinary research focuses on the design, development, and evaluation of advanced learning technologies—such as intelligent tutoring systems, serious games, simulation, immersive virtual environment, Azevedo leads several federally funded projects, including those supported by the NSF, DOE, IES, and NIH, and has published extensively on cognitive, affective, and metacognitive processes during learning, reasoning, and problem solving.
