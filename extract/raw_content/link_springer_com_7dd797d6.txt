Abstract
--------

Artificial intelligence (AI) technology is increasingly applied in education. This meta-analysis synthesizes findings from 49 controlled experiments (including randomized controlled trials and quasi-experimental designs) to examine the effects of AI-assisted learning on student performance. Using a random-effects model with trim-and-fill correction for publication bias, the pooled effect size was 0.449 (95% CI: [0.194, 0.704]). AI-assisted learning significantly improves learning attitude, achievement, and motivation compared to traditional instruction, with effects varying across educational levels. Future research should explore moderators, cognitive skills, and cross-cultural implementation.
