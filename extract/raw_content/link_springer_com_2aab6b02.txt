Abstract
--------

Information and communications technology competency (ICT-C) has been widely recognized as crucial for enhancing K12 teachers’ teaching quality and fostering their career development in 21st-century education. Previous studies have explored the positive effects of both external and internal factors on teachers’ ICT-C. However, the underlying mechanisms through which these factors influence ICT-C have not been fully revealed. Particularly, the direct and indirect pathways linking ICT self-concept (ICT-SC), ICT self-efficacy (ICT-SE), and ICT-C, as well as the moderating roles of individual differences in these relationships, remain largely unexplored in the current literature. To examine the potential pathways among these factors, data collected from 280 teachers were analyzed. Confirmatory factor analysis (CFA) was conducted to validate the measurement instruments, and structural equation modeling (SEM) was used to examine the hypothesized relationships. Results indicated that K12 teachers’ ICT-SC significantly predicts their ICT-C, suggesting that teachers who perceive themselves as more capable of using ICT tend to demonstrate higher ICT-C. K12 teachers’ ICT-SE mediated the relationship between ICT-SC and ICT-C, indicating that strengthening teachers’ ICT-SE is a key approach to transforming their broader ICT-SC into effective technology-integrated teaching practices. Additionally, this mediating effect was not significantly moderated by gender, teaching experience or their interaction. This study provides empirical evidence supporting both theoretical frameworks and practical strategies for promoting the integration and development of K12 teachers’ ICT-C.

Appendices

### Appendix 1. ICT-SC scale with 4 items

| Item | Content |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | I can restore the functionality of digital systems in case of problems without the help of others. |
| 2 | I am good at solving problems of digital systems without the help of others. |
| 3 | I quickly learn to solve content problems with the help of digital systems. |
| 4 | It is easy for me to select suitable digital systems and to solve content problems. |

### Appendix 2. ICT-SE scale with 4 items

| Item | Content |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | I search, evaluate and select appropriate IT resources to support lesson activities. |
| 2 | I am able to adopt and adapt given IT-based learning activities. |
| 3 | I can manage IT-based learning activities in a computer laboratory. |
| 4 | I am able to adopt and adapt activities that incorporate the use of IT to assess pupils’ learning and provide immediate and constructive feedback. |

### Appendix 3. ICT-C scale with 22 items

| Construct | Item | Content |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Instructional resource development competence | 1 | I am able to search, filter, and integrate learning resources according to teaching needs. |
| 2 | I am able to process and develop online teaching resources (e.g., video editing) based on teaching objectives and content. |
| 3 | I am able to flexibly select different ICT tools according to teaching objectives and content, and design and produce multimedia teaching resources (e.g., multimedia courseware, micro-lessons, recorded lessons). |
| 4 | I am able to select or build appropriate learning platforms for students (e.g., cloud classrooms, online learning platforms). |
| Instructional design competence | 5 | I am able to use visualization tools such as mind maps to design teaching content. |
| 6 | To help students better understand key and difficult points, I am able to use ICT to provide learning resources and reorganize teaching content. |
| 7 | I am able to select appropriate instructional media and ICT tools according to teaching objectives and content. |
| 8 | I am able to design an ICT-based instructional process that effectively achieves learning objectives. |
| 9 | I am able to promptly handle unexpected situations that occur during the application of ICT. |
| Instructional implementation competence | 10 | I often use subject-related websites or online learning platforms (e.g., Zhixue.com) to guide and support students’ learning. |
| 11 | I often use ICT tools to collect students’ feedback (e.g., receiving assignments or questions online through platforms). |
| 12 | When students encounter problems during ICT-based learning activities (e.g., software or communication platform failures), I am able to appropriately adjust the teaching activities. |
| 13 | In teaching activities, I am able to make full use of ICT to create learning activities that promote students’ innovation, collaboration, communication, and critical thinking skills. |
| Instructional assessment competence | 14 | I am able to design diverse assessment schemes (e.g., tests, portfolios, rubrics) by selecting appropriate ICT tools according to curriculum standards and learning objectives. |
| 15 | I am able to use ICT tools to conduct online learning and testing. |
| 16 | I am proficient in using ICT tools (e.g., Excel, SPSS) to collect, analyze, and interpret students’ performance data. |
| 17 | I am proficient in using ICT tools (e.g., QQ groups) to conduct formative and summative assessments of students’ learning. |
| 18 | I am able to use ICT tools to enable students to engage in self-assessment, peer assessment, or group evaluation by reporting their learning processes and outcomes. |
| professional learning and development competence | 19 | I often take the initiative to use ICT for self-reflection and professional development (e.g., watching my own recorded lessons, keeping online teaching reflection journals). |
| 20 | I often improve my professional competence through blended learning approaches that combine online and offline learning (e.g., online teacher learning communities, offline teaching research activities). |
| 21 | I often use ICT to communicate with colleagues inside and outside the school to exchange experiences and stay updated on the latest developments in teaching (e.g., study tours, online communities). |
| 22 | I often use ICT tools and online resource databases to conduct subject-based research and write academic papers. |
