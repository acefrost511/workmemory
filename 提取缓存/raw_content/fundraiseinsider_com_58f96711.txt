How to Sell to Education and EdTech Startups
--------------------------------------------

### 1. Why should I target recently funded education and EdTech startups?

Education and EdTech startups that have just raised funding are under pressure to grow quickly and deliver measurable results. They are actively looking for expert partners to help build technology, improve learning outcomes, expand into new markets, and increase adoption by schools, students, or organizations.


### 2. What kinds of startups are considered education and EdTech companies?

Education startups include companies that focus on:

* Online learning platforms for students or professionals
* Learning management systems and digital classrooms
* Skills training and career development platforms
* Education games and interactive content tools
* Curriculum development for schools and districts
* Test preparation, tutoring, and assessment services

### 3. How do I find recently funded education startups?

You can subscribe to our [B2B sales leads list](https://fundraiseinsider.com/) for weekly updates on education and EdTech startups that have secured funding. These updates include verified contact details for decision makers such as founders, product leads, and operations executives.


### 4. Who should I contact inside these startups?

Your outreach will depend on your offer, but ideal contacts include:

* CTO, Head of Product, Platform Manager
* Curriculum or content support: Chief Learning Officer, Director of Education
* Marketing and growth: CMO, Growth Lead, Community Manager
* Sales or partnerships: CRO, Business Development Lead

### 5. What are their top priorities after raising funding?

Education startups that recently raised capital are often focused on:

* Developing new features or building mobile and web platforms
* Creating scalable content for new subjects or audiences
* Improving student or educator engagement and retention
* Hiring teams to support product, marketing, and support functions
* Expanding to international or underserved education markets

### 6. What types of vendors are education and EdTech startups hiring?

These startups often work with:

* [Custom software development firms](https://fundraiseinsider.com/lead-generation-software-companies/) with EdTech experience
* Instructional designers and curriculum writers
* Video and animation studios for learning content
* Marketing and user acquisition agencies
* [Recruiters](https://fundraiseinsider.com/recruitment-leads/) and [staffing agencies](https://fundraiseinsider.com/staffing-agency-leads/) for education technology and learning design roles

### 7. When is the best time to contact them?

The best time to contact an education startup is within 1 to 2 weeks after their funding is announced. This is when they are building new roadmaps, onboarding new team members, and seeking vendors who can help them move faster.


### 8. What makes education and EdTech startups different from other types of companies?

Education startups must often balance business growth with measurable learning impact. They serve diverse users such as teachers, students, administrators, and parents. Understanding how to support outcomes like retention, mastery, and engagement is key when presenting your solution.


### 9. How should I pitch my product or service to them?

Your pitch should be clear, relevant, and focused on results. Mention their recent raise, show how your solution supports their growth goals, and provide a case study if possible. Education companies value both innovation and trust.

*Example:* “Congratulations on the $5 million raise. We helped a coding education platform improve student retention by 42% in 90 days. I put together 3 suggestions based on your site if you are exploring ways to grow engagement.”

Yes. [Fundraise Insider](https://fundraiseinsider.com/pricing/) sends weekly updates that include education startups which have recently raised funding, along with contact information for the right people to reach out to.
