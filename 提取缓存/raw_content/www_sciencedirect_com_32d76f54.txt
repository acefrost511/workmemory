## ABSTRACT
As multi-course microcredentials become central to workforce development, they require learners to sustain self-regulated effort over extended periods. Most learning analytics research often relies on static models focused on dropout versus completion, without distinguishing between strong mastery and minimal performance among those who finish. Grounded in Self-Regulated Learning (SRL) and Temporal Learning Analytics (TLA), this study examines the longitudinal journey of 885 program completers, divided into high- and low-achievement groups. We combined longitudinal clustering, modality-specific logistic regression, random forest modeling, and differential sequential pattern mining to identify patterns linked to higher achievement. Results show a clear predictive asymmetry in the forethought phase. Early active enactment in graded tasks, such as completing a peer assessment, increased the odds of high achievement by approximately 33 times (OR = 32.85), whereas passive consumption showed no significant effect. Across the full program, major graded assessments were the primary turning points. A Full Journey Random Forest model (ROC-AUC = 0.975) confirmed that summative milestones, rather than formative activities, most clearly separated high and low achievers. Sequential pattern mining showed no single pathway to success. Instead, high achievers followed six distinct but coherent patterns (Lift > 10) that varied in formative engagement yet consistently converged on summative requirements. In contrast, low achievers showed fragmented and inconsistent sequences. These findings support a model of structured flexibility within the context of this professional microcredential program: sustained success depends on steady completion of key graded milestones alongside adaptive management of surrounding learning activities.

## The Challenge of Longitudinal Persistence in Microcredentials
The term microcredential is broad, with consensus on its definition only recently emerging (Bideau & Kearns, 2022; Tamoliune et al., 2023). The landscape is diverse, covering a wide range of offerings from single-course digital badges and bite-sized competency assessments (Ha et al., 2023) to more extensive, multi-course certifications (McGreal & Olcott, 2022).
