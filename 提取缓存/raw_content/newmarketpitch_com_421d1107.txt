What are the latest funding news in the EdTech market? (March 2026)

The EdTech market saw a strong wave of funding activity between October 2025 and late February 2026, with deals spanning K-12 curriculum platforms, AI tutoring tools, workforce learning, and exam integrity software.

From a $150M unicorn round for Preply to pre-seed bets on career guidance for Indian teenagers, investors are backing a wide range of education use cases across geographies and company stages.

AI is the common thread running through nearly every deal, whether applied to personalized tutoring, teacher productivity, course authoring, or school administration.

And if you want to better understand this new industry, **[you can download our pitch covering the EdTech market](https://newmarketpitch.com/pages/edtech)**.


**Insights**

* AI is present in virtually every deal on this list, but the application varies widely: from adaptive tutoring engines and curriculum delivery to exam proctoring and teacher admin tools, suggesting the EdTech AI wave is horizontal, not concentrated in one category.
* The largest round, Preply's $150M Series D at a $1.2B valuation, is the only unicorn in the batch and stands out as profitable, a rare signal in a cohort dominated by seed and early-stage companies.
* India accounts for three deals (Emversity, Uolo, MU20), confirming it remains one of the most active EdTech geographies globally, with a mix of workforce training, school curriculum, and career readiness companies attracting capital.
* K-12 is the most represented segment with five companies (Subject, Kinderpedia, Vimi, Sparkli, Uolo) raising across curriculum, tutoring, school ops, and learning apps, reflecting sustained investor interest in the school market.
* The workforce and adult learning segment attracted two notable deals (Scholé AI at $3M and Emversity at $30M), pointing to continued appetite for upskilling platforms as AI reshapes job roles.
* Several rounds are notably small: MU20 raised just $500K and Teacher's Buddy raised $1.85M, suggesting that even modest capital is enough to validate early EdTech concepts when the problem is specific and the team is credible.
* Exam integrity platform Invigilator raised $11M in equity funding with an explicit U.S. expansion mandate, a sign that assessment and proctoring infrastructure is becoming a priority as online education scales.
* Most deals did not disclose a valuation, with only Preply ($1.2B) and Emversity (~$120M post-money) being transparent, reflecting the broader trend of valuation opacity at early stages.
* Geographic diversity in this batch is notable, with deals from the U.S., India, Romania, Switzerland, Australia, and globally focused companies, suggesting EdTech investment is not concentrated in a single market.
