## ABSTRACT
The integration of artificial intelligence into education remains challenged by issues of scalability, interpretability, and multimodal adaptability. DeepSeek's AI-driven educational tools show potential to improve educational applications through advances in reasoning efficiency, lightweight deployment, and multimodal fusion. This paper explores the DeepSeek model family and its transformative applications across five core educational scenarios, including learning, teaching, teaching research, management, and evaluation. Despite challenges such as excessive dependence on structured reasoning pathways, hallucination risks, sustainability of localized deployment, and limited ability to process multimodal data in real time, DeepSeek demonstrates significant potential in advancing equitable, intelligent, and human-centric educational ecosystems with ethical governance. Future development priorities include responsible AI frameworks and the shift of educational stakeholders' roles to cultivate equitable, adaptive educational ecosystems with the guidance of educational policies and further breakthroughs in technologies.
## 1 Introduction
The integration of artificial intelligence (AI) into education has driven transformative changes, yet widespread adoption remains constrained by challenges in scalability, interpretability, and multimodal adaptability. Conventional AI models often suffer from opaque decision-making, high computational demands, and limited capacity to process cross-modal data, which undermine their effectiveness in dynamic learning environments.
The DeepSeek model family is an open-source suite of large-scale language models designed to advance artificial general intelligence (AGI) and to compete with closed-source alternatives in performance and cost-effectiveness. DeepSeek models have evolved rapidly from DeepSeek-V1's foundational code generation capabilities to DeepSeek-V2.5's enhanced mathematical reasoning and real-time search and the highly efficient DeepSeek-V3 for long text and competitive tasks. The DeepSeek-R1 series, powered by reinforcement learning, aims to improve reasoning and multimodal integration. Moreover, Janus-Pro is a cross-modal system built upon DeepSeek-R1's architecture, enabling seamless integration of text, code, visual, and contextual data.
This paper examines DeepSeek's technological framework, including the deep thinking mechanisms of the DeepSeek-R1 model, resource optimization, cross-modal integration in the Janus-Pro system, and their implications for educational transformation. Through analysis of applications in adaptive learning systems, instructional support mechanisms, and metacognitive assessment frameworks, we demonstrate DeepSeek's capacity to transition education from standardized instruction to personalized and context-sensitive ecosystems. This study further addresses critical challenges, including hallucination risks and ethical considerations, proposing a developmental roadmap for responsible AI integration. Combining technical analysis with empirical case studies, this research highlights DeepSeek's potential to democratize educational access while advocating for continued advancement in hybrid intelligence systems and ethical AI practices.
## 2 The Breakthrough Innovation of DeepSeek
### 2.1 Core Technological Breakthroughs of DeepSeek
DeepSeek represents significant progress in AI development, particularly through enhanced reasoning efficiency and complex task management capabilities. The DeepSeek-R1 model employs chain-of-thought (CoT) mechanisms and reinforcement learning techniques to optimize reasoning tasks and show strong performance in long-text analysis and multistep problem-solving scenarios (DeepSeek-Al et al. [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0007)). Through lightweight deployment technologies, including mixture of experts (MoE) architectures, Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO), and knowledge distillation techniques, these methods reduce computational demands in many settings while maintaining operational efficiency across diverse hardware environments. The Janus-Pro multimodal model advances cross-modal integration through deep fusion of textual, visual, and auditory data streams, enabling sophisticated understanding and generation capabilities across heterogeneous data types. These methods may be applicable to a range of educational tasks, depending on context and requirements, while also serving as technical foundations for future AI applications.
#### 2.1.1 Deep Thinking Model
The DeepSeek-R1 model has made significant advancements in AI by incorporating deep reasoning capabilities and reinforcement learning mechanisms. Its reasoning architecture efficiently handles complex causal relationships, particularly excelling at analyzing long content and solving multistep problems. By using multihead latent attention mechanisms, the model improves computational efficiency when dealing with large-scale textual data and complex tasks (H. M. Zhang [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0046)). Additionally, the use of low-rank decomposition techniques enhances the model's applications in education, such as essay evaluation and interdisciplinary knowledge integration (Liu, Feng, Xue, et al. [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0025); Liu, Feng, Wang, et al. [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0024)). The model also possesses cross-modal reasoning abilities, enabling the effective integration of different data types, including text, images, and audio, making it applicable in various fields, such as autonomous systems and medical diagnostics.
Central to the model's innovation is its reinforcement learning mechanism, which dynamically adjusts learning strategies based on task requirements and data characteristics (DeepSeek-Al et al. [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0007)). This flexibility enhances generalization capabilities while addressing challenges intractable to conventional models. The system's robust learning capacity maintains high efficiency across diverse problem types, significantly improving adaptability in complex educational scenarios.
In conclusion, the DeepSeek-R1 architecture suggests a shift in the approach to autonomous reasoning and decision-making. This system transcends conventional task-specific AI implementations, emerging as an intelligent framework capable of complex analytical processes with broad implications for educational innovation.
#### 2.1.2 Lightweight Models
DeepSeek's lightweight models combine multiple optimization technologies to balance computational efficiency with performance integrity. The integration of MoE architectures, GRPO optimization, MLA (Multi-head Latent Attention) mechanisms, and multi-token prediction (MTP) systems enables substantial resource reduction while maintaining high task performance.
The MoE architecture employs sparse activation strategies through dynamic gating mechanisms, selectively engaging expert modules to reduce computational load. This architecture enables efficient large-scale data processing while providing flexible personalized learning support through task-specific expert specialization (Liu, Feng, Xue, et al. [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0025); Liu, Feng, Wang, et al. [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0024)).
GRPO technology eliminates traditional value models through multisample generation methods and reward model optimization, reducing resource consumption while improving inference accuracy. This approach enables dynamic content adaptation based on learner progression, facilitating personalized education pathways (Jahin et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0015)).
MTP technology enhances output quality through simultaneous multi-token prediction, improving accuracy and diversity in generation tasks. This capability optimizes intelligent tutoring systems and programming education support through efficient resolution of mathematical and computational challenges (Neha and Bhati [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0028)).
Knowledge distillation techniques enable efficient knowledge transfer from large-scale models to compact implementations, preserving performance while reducing hardware requirements. This approach facilitates low-latency personalized learning experiences, particularly in environments with constrained resources (Duan [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0011)).
Collectively, these technologies enable efficient operation across diverse hardware platforms, significantly reducing computational costs while expanding educational technology accessibility.
#### 2.1.3 Multimodal Fusion
The Janus-Pro multimodal model advances AI by integrating understanding and generation capabilities across text, images, video, and audio. The system processes different types of data through adaptive strategies, prioritizing the most relevant ones during reasoning. It can generate specific outputs based on combined understanding, enabling tasks such as creating images from text descriptions or synthesizing text from audiovisual inputs (X. Chen et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0005)). The model also uses cross-modal learning to integrate complementary information from different data types. This approach improves reasoning and optimizes generation tasks by adapting to each modality. These innovations allow the model to produce high-quality multimodal outputs with consistent meaning across different formats, showing strong performance in complex tasks such as health care diagnostics and interdisciplinary education.
### 2.2 Core Advantages of DeepSeek in Educational Application
DeepSeek’s technological innovations create transformative educational impacts through enhanced interpretability, resource-efficient deployment, and cross-modal applications. These advancements improve system intelligence, personalize learning experiences, and optimize interactions between teachers and students across diverse learning environments. The following sections detail these educational advantages.
#### 2.2.1 Enhanced Interpretability
DeepSeek’s deep thinking framework provides transparent decision-making processes that increase educator trust and student comprehension. In intelligent tutoring systems, the technology enables full traceability of analytical processes, allowing educators to verify AI-generated recommendations and adjust pedagogical strategies accordingly (Kotsis [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0018)). Students benefit from detailed feedback mechanisms that illuminate problem-solving pathways and knowledge gaps, particularly in STEM disciplines requiring sequential reasoning. This transparency promotes metacognitive development by enabling learners to analyze their cognitive processes systematically.
#### 2.2.2 Resource-Efficient Deployment
DeepSeek’s resource-efficient architecture enables widespread adoption through edge computing capabilities. The system’s optimized performance on low-cost devices demonstrates particular significance for rural and educational institutions with limited resources (Xiao et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0043)). Localized deployment ensures data security and real-time responsiveness, supporting dynamic instructional adjustments based on learner performance metrics.
#### 2.2.3 Cross-Modal Application in Education
DeepSeek’s multimodal integration transforms educational experiences through synchronized analysis of textual, visual, and auditory data streams. Cross-modal generation capabilities enhance knowledge representation through dynamic visualizations and interactive content, particularly benefiting abstract concept mastery in mathematics and scientific disciplines (M. Zhou et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0050)). This multimodal approach accommodates diverse learning preferences while improving knowledge retention and engagement metrics.
## 3 DeepSeek’s Paradigm Reconstruction in Education Scenarios
### 3.1 Learning Scenarios: From Uniform Indoctrination to Adaptive Cognitive Navigation
Traditional learning in a classroom is fixed without sufficient contextual information. The learning content is uniform and limited by fixed class hours. The teacher–student relationship presents the characteristics of unidirectional control, suppressing students’ active construction and differentiated development needs. However, the learning systems based on traditional AI suffer from a significant shortage of transparency, as the reasoning process of the AI models is in a “black box.” For example, the logic of learning path planning is difficult to be clarified. This lack of transparency hinders students’ ability to internalize the knowledge, as students are unable to reconstruct the complete cognitive chain through the output of the system, resulting in the internalization of knowledge at the level of mechanical imitation (George [2023](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0012)). With the three core advantages of enhanced interpretability, lightweight deployment, and cross-modality, DeepSeek plays a multidimensional revolutionary role in learning scenarios.
#### 3.1.1 Optimizing Learning Understanding and Trustworthiness With Enhanced Interpretability
DeepSeek’s enhanced interpretability helps learners penetrate the reasoning process of the black box and visualize the logical chain-of-knowledge construction through interpretable AI techniques. For example, in mathematics education, DeepSeekMath is able to demonstrate the logic of problem-solving at a level near human, enabling students not only to access the answers but also to progressively understand the paths of derivation and to enhance their mastery of abstract concepts (Shao et al. [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0035)). DeepSeekMath is a large-scale language model designed specifically for mathematical reasoning, which is significantly enhanced through large-scale pretraining and instruction tuning to significantly improve mathematical reasoning. For example, in the MATH benchmark test, the DeepSeekMath-Base model achieves an accuracy of 51.7%, which is close to the state of the art, and its enhanced version, DeepSeekMath-Instruct, even outperforms the performance of GPT-4 on some tasks (Shao et al. [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0035)).
#### 3.1.2 Personalized Learning With Lower Resources
DeepSeek adopts efficient model architectures to dramatically reduce computational resource requirements, lowering the accessibility threshold for intelligent educational tools while advancing equitable resource distribution. The MoE architecture enables granular personalization by dynamically routing student data to domain expert modules, thereby tailoring content sequencing without increasing inference costs. For instance, DeepSeek-V2 achieves a 42.5% reduction in training costs and a 93.3% compression of Key-Value cache compared to its predecessor while supporting 128K-long contextual analysis (DeepSeek-Al et al. [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0007)). This efficiency allows the model to perform real-time, individualized scaffolding, such as adjusting problem difficulty curves based on a student’s response latency and error types, even on low-cost devices. The architecture’s lean footprint enables seamless operation on common hardware, particularly in regions with constrained resources. For example, a DeepSeek system, deployed locally, can track user interests and generate personalized dialogues in real time to enhance learning engagement (G. Zhang et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0044); H. Zhang et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0045)). The system consists of an image extraction module that tracks interests in real time and an LLM-driven module that balances personalization and fidelity to facilitate two-way cognitive interactions that go beyond traditional recommendation logic.
#### 3.1.3 Building an Immersive Learning Ecology With Multimodality
Multimodal interaction support includes integrating text, image, and code generation for visualization in STEM subjects and providing API interfaces to empower universities to develop customized private models. Unlike general AI tools, DeepSeek’s unique advantage lies in its role flexibility and the integration of educational theories, which enables a deep synergy between technological tools and pedagogical methods and provides localized and specialized AI solutions for higher education scenarios (Ding et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0010)). In terms of personalized learning navigation, DeepSeek can diagnose knowledge gaps, generate targeted remediation plans, recommend differentiated resources based on cognitive styles, and facilitate metacognitive reflection through guided questioning. For example, based on DeepSeek-VL’s multimodal fusion capability, it builds subject knowledge maps. When students make frequent mistakes in math application problems, the system automatically correlates historical data of language reading comprehension and identifies the root cause of question misunderstanding (Q. Jiang et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0016); Z. Jiang et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0017)). Using semantic analysis of DeepSeek-VL, cross-domain knowledge is extracted from ancient library texts or medical patient education materials to generate customized reinforcement modules (M. Zhou et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0050)).
The integration of DeepSeek’s multimodal reasoning, resource-efficient architectures, and transparent decision pathways redefines learning as an adaptive cognitive journey. By transforming static content delivery into context-sensitive knowledge navigation, these innovations enable learners to actively reconstruct cognitive chains while preserving cultural and ethical values inherent in educational ecosystems.
### 3.2 Teaching Scenarios: From Experience-Driven to Mind Visualization Interventions
Traditional teaching models often rely on experience-driven lesson plans and delayed diagnostic feedback, creating inefficiencies in the observe-analyze-revise pedagogical cycle. Although AI tools have introduced dynamic content adaptation and automated assessment capabilities, conventional systems exhibit three critical limitations, including decision-making processes remaining opaque, struggling to deliver real-time feedback in low-connectivity environments due to centralized computational architectures, and analytics treating textual, visual, and behavioral data as isolated streams rather than interactively correlated inputs (Denny et al. [2023](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0008)). DeepSeek addresses these gaps through integrated technological innovations that enable real-time cognitive process visualization and adaptive instruction.
#### 3.2.1 Interpretable AI Teaching Assistant
Relying on the advantage of enhanced interpretability technology, DeepSeek optimizes the knowledge retrieval process by integrating structured knowledge and explainable AI algorithms to effectively improve the cognitive support effectiveness of the teaching support system. For example, DeepSeek-V3 optimizes the ratio of mathematical and programming samples, and it can quickly respond to the professional query needs of the teacher's preparation for the classroom, together with the change in the requirements for the output of the model, which requires that the model respond to the question by generating the answer and the inference process simultaneously, and the teacher can intuitively respond to the answer and inference process and verify the reliability of the AI recommendation results (Xiao et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0043)). Its multilingual support on 128K word lists in addition to Chinese and English is especially suitable for internationalized teaching scenarios.
#### 3.2.2 AI-Supported Teaching With Lower Resources for Schools in Rural Areas
DeepSeek, with its lightweight deployment advantage, can revolutionize teaching strategies in environments with low connectivity. For example, the offline intelligent tutor can rely on DeepSeek's local lightweight deployment capability, implant the lightweight AI model into the tablet/learning machine terminal, apply it to the rural classroom, and use the offline terminal to run the intelligent system, assisting the teacher to complete the real-time modification of the classroom exercises and hierarchical guidance. As a result, DeepSeek's lightweight deployment provides schools in rural areas with a viable path for AI-assisted teaching, providing strong support for the modernization and development of rural education by optimizing the use of resources, improving teaching efficiency, and promoting educational equity (Li [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0022)). In addition, DeepSeek can provide a data middleware solution for primary and secondary education through low-cost and secure localized deployment. Its core advantage lies in low-cost private deployment; that is, ordinary PCs can satisfy the hardware requirements of the non-full-blooded version of DeepSeek, such as the 7B, 14B, and 32B models, whereas the data are completely privatized and stored in the local area network, which ensures the security of the data.
#### 3.2.3 Multimodal Teaching Progress Feedback
DeepSeek provides important support for the innovation of teaching mode through its multimodal processing capability and intelligent interaction function. For example, a multimodal analysis method integrating classroom expressions in visual modality and assignment data in textual modality is now proposed to realize dynamic group teaching in K12 education. Students' facial data are captured in real time by classroom cameras, combined with assignment error patterns, and a knowledge graph can be used to construct students' subgroups. The system can provide real-time feedback of grouping suggestions through edge computing to dynamically adjust the teaching content. Throughout the process, DeepSeek reconstructs the teacher's preparation mode through intelligent tools. It automatically generates subject knowledge maps based on the keywords of the course topics to organize the logical structure of the teaching content, develops multimodal resources, and integrates multilingual databases to provide localized materials for cross-cultural courses. In addition, DeepSeek is able to help students better understand complex physics concepts through the combination of visual and verbal language, thus enhancing teaching effectiveness (Kotsis [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0018)).
By synchronizing multimodal insights with pedagogical intent, DeepSeek transforms teaching from intuition-based practice to evidence-guided mentorship. The convergence of real-time cognitive mapping, equitable resource distribution, and multimodal analytics empowers educators to visualize and respond to learner needs at previously unattainable temporal and spatial resolutions.
### 3.3 Teaching Research Scenarios: From Simple Attribution to Teaching-Strategy Labs
Teaching research, a cornerstone of pedagogical professionalization in China's basic education system (Shen and Yang [2019](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0036)), remains constrained by an empirical paradigm characterized by ritualized lesson observation cycles and superficial collective lesson preparation. Current practices exhibit limited capacity to stimulate cognitive collisions among educators and diagnose authentic classroom dilemmas to refine pedagogical strategies (Lu and Liu [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0026)). This epistemic stagnation is exacerbated in interdisciplinary contexts, where overreliance on individual knowledge reservoirs undermines the observation-imitation-collaboration mechanisms fundamental to alternative learning theory (Z. H. Chen [2003](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0006)).
In recent years, the application of large language models (LLM) in teaching research scenarios has expanded from instructional feedback generation to contextual simulation training and teacher professional development support. For example, R. Wang and Demszky ([2023](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0040)) used ChatGPT to provide teaching feedback such as classroom ratings, highlight identification, and suggestions for improvement. Holster et al. generated dynamic classroom scenarios and reproduced complex classroom events through a large language model to provide teachers with immersive teaching and research training and to assist teachers in their professional development (Holster et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0013)). Wang's empirical study builds an LLM-supported blended teacher development model, demonstrating the application of LLM in the whole process of teaching and research diagnosis, personalized feedback, and blended training (X. Wang et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0041)).
Although conventional LLMs have initiated a shift toward data-driven teaching research by modeling causal relationships between instructional behaviors and learning outcomes, they suffer from three systemic limitations: (1) output quality dependency on training data representativeness, often yielding pedagogically inert suggestions divorced from localized classroom realities; (2) failure to formalize tacit teaching expertise into actionable, domain-structured strategies; and (3) inability to sustain context-aware, iterative improvement cycles.
DeepSeek addresses these gaps by enabling dynamic assessment of instructional efficacy (Xiao et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0043)). Its cross-modal fusion engine correlates real-time classroom discourse analysis, student engagement metrics, and knowledge graphs aligned to curriculum to generate situated recommendations.
#### 3.3.1 Interpretable Teaching Research
One of the core strengths of CoT-based LLMs such as DeepSeek in the field of teaching and research is their ability to present complex analytical processes in an analytically transparent and interpretable way that enhances teachers' trust and promotes their professional reflection. X. Wang et al. ([2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0041)) implemented a blended teacher professional development program empowered by an LLM, using focus group interviews and quantitative analysis, and found that the program enabled targeted training, offered personalized learning experiences, and improved teachers' competency for instructional design in blended learning, demonstrating that LLMs effectively empowered teachers' professional development. M. Li et al. ([2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0021)) proposed a structured instructional design framework, ARCHED, in which the objective analysis engine module mentions the use of CoT reasoning to analyze whether the instructional objectives are in line with the instructional principles and to form interpretable instructional design feedback step by step. This staged reasoning mechanism allows teachers to clearly understand the basis and analysis path of each objective so that they can make more data-driven teaching decisions, and they can also learn from the analysis ideas, which will help their personal growth.
#### 3.3.2 Personalized Teaching Professional Development With Lightweight Deployment
DeepSeek's lightweight deployment framework addresses critical gaps in equitable access to AI-enhanced teaching professional development, enabling resource-constrained schools to implement personalized pedagogical innovation through teaching research. By dynamically adapting to individual teaching styles, such as generating questioning templates for teachers favoring Socratic methods or structured concept maps for instructors, the system bridges the divide between institutional resource limitations and evidence-based professional development (Shen and Yang [2019](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0036); Lu and Liu [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0026)). Its capacity to analyze multi-lesson instructional sequences helps teachers identify systemic issues in curriculum design, such as misaligned scaffolding in physics units or disjointed literacy skill progression (Z. H. Chen [2003](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0006)). Case studies in rural classrooms also demonstrate how localized deployment supports real-time strategy optimization while maintaining compliance with data privacy regulations (Kotsis [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0018); Xiao et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0043)). This architecture transforms ordinary hardware into collaborative pedagogical laboratories, allowing teachers to iteratively refine practices using evidence without reliance on cloud infrastructure (Denny et al. [2023](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0008)).
#### 3.3.3 Cross-Modal Teaching Research
Breaking through the simple text analysis, the LLM is rapidly developing in the direction of multimodality, enabling it to integrate and process a variety of information such as text, images, audio, and video. This cross-modal comprehension and analysis capability provides unprecedented technical support for gaining insight into complex real-life teaching situations, enabling the construction of a more comprehensive perception of teaching activities. Formative assessment is moving from a single text to a multimodal expression of text, graphs, and hand drawings (Nguyen and Park [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0029)). In terms of online learning, screen analysis can be used to understand students' learning behaviors and actions (G. Zhang et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0044); H. Zhang et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0045)). In addition, multimodal LLMs can be used in the analysis of real classroom teaching to understand semantics such as who is discussing at the top table, who is holding his hand up to his face, etc. (Whitehead et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0042)). Multimodal large models can also fully understand and merge the multimodal information of the teaching scene so that they can provide teachers with a comprehensive analysis of the content of the research. For example, Lv ([2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0027)) embedded DeepSeek models into the whole cycle of teaching and learning on the Chaoxing platform, automatically generating lesson plans through natural language processing (NLP) and using NLP to create a new classroom scenario. It has been used to automatically generate lesson plans through NLP, enhance classroom engagement through real-time voice interaction, and achieve precise teaching and research interventions using a multimodal early warning model.
In summary, DeepSeek repositions teaching research as a laboratory for iterative pedagogical innovation. Through multimodal data triangulation and adaptive strategy generation, the system transforms isolated teaching acts into interconnected nodes within a continuously evolving educational knowledge graph.
### 3.4 Management Scenarios: From Empirical Decision-Making to Educational Eco-Simulators
The traditional education management model emphasizes standardized teaching and centralized management, where educational resources are uniformly allocated and controlled by the school leadership, teachers mainly play the role of knowledge transmitters, and students are passive knowledge receivers. This model focuses on the unity of curriculum and consistency of teaching in the process of implementation, which facilitates centralized supervision and evaluation by administrators. However, with the diversification of social needs and the rapid development of technology, the traditional model gradually shows its limitations and is difficult to meet the needs of individualization and flexibility (H. Chen [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0004)). Administration relies on manual statistics and empirical decision-making, and the risk-warning capability is weak. The application of DeepSeek in education management can significantly improve the efficiency and accuracy of decision-making.
#### 3.4.1 Transparent Decision-Making With Enhanced Interpretability
DeepSeek models can provide intelligent support for education management systems leveraging their strong language comprehension and reasoning. For example, DeepSeek-R1 is able to provide a clearer basis for decision-making in scenarios such as teaching evaluation and student behavior analysis (C. Wang and Kantarcioglu [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0038)). Examples include using DeepSeek to build an eco-simulation platform, support decision-making in educational systems, enable collaboration, and explore educational governance models. They use DeepSeek not only as a natural language processing tool but also as a central intelligence engine for simulating intelligent ecosystems, helping educators analyze the effectiveness of teaching content and students' learning habits, thereby optimizing curriculum design and teaching methods (X. Zhou [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0051)).
#### 3.4.2 Resource Efficiency and Data Security
DeepSeek's low-resource architecture revolutionizes educational management by enabling equitable access to AI-driven decision-making tools in resource-constrained environments. Its lightweight models reduce computational demands compared to conventional systems (DeepSeek-Al et al. [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0007)), allowing deployment on legacy school servers and tablets, which are critical for rural or underfunded districts. This efficiency facilitates real-time, localized analysis of administrative workflows. For example, principals can optimize faculty deployment by modeling classroom performance trends in a semester, whereas district administrators detect infrastructure risks. In addition, DeepSeek's localized deployment model fundamentally redefines data security in educational management by ensuring sensitive information remains entirely within institutional control. Unlike cloud systems that risk exposing student records and operational data to third-party vulnerabilities, DeepSeek operates exclusively within school or district networks, eliminating external data transmission. For instance, Shandong University in China adopts an innovative integration model of a local knowledge base and DeepSeek-R1 on campus, leveraging the powerful capabilities of the DeepSeek-R1 model to achieve precise retrieval, in-depth analysis, and efficient summarization for the exclusive knowledge base of faculty and students (Shandong University [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0034)). The application of DeepSeek-R1 provides intelligent, user-friendly, and efficient support services for document processing in daily administrative tasks and the integration of materials for teaching and research.
#### 3.4.3 Cross-Modal Intelligent Management
DeepSeek's multimodal integration capabilities, particularly exemplified by Janus-Pro's unified image and text processing, significantly enhance evidence-based educational governance. The system generates granular insights into curriculum efficacy and pedagogical gaps by analyzing and correlating diverse instructional artifacts, such as computer science exercises with diagrams, annotated programming screenshots, and cybersecurity problem logs. For instance, Janus-Pro's high-resolution visual coding enables administrators to evaluate the alignment between theoretical concepts and students' practical implementation skills in computer science education management. This capability proved instrumental in a large-scale evaluation study, where the system processed exercises and real-world cybersecurity scenarios to identify systemic issues such as inconsistent rubric application across programming modules (Xiao et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0043)). Crucially, its cross-linguistic analysis revealed disparities in non-native English speakers' comprehension of technical documentation, prompting curriculum teams to redesign multilingual learning resources.
Moreover, the Academic Affairs Office of Beijing Institute of Technology has customized the DeepSeek large model to develop a model specifically designed for undergraduate academic management scenarios as SmartEdu (Beijing Institute of Technology Academic Affairs Office [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0002)). SmartEdu overcomes the limitations of general large models in vertical domain applications, demonstrating unique advantages in cross-modal intelligent management. The model supports an intelligent customer service function capable of responding to academic affairs inquiries, as well as several business processes such as teaching quality data analysis and visualization and intelligent PPT management. Through cross-modal integration, SmartEdu can consolidate and analyze information from various forms, including text, images, and data, thereby enhancing the accuracy and efficiency of management and decision-making.
In summary, DeepSeek redefines educational leadership, shifting management practices from intuitive oversight to multimodal, logic decision frameworks. By transmuting administrative intuition into multimodal predictive analytics, DeepSeek positions educational management as a precision science. The shift from reactive resource allocation to proactive ecosystem simulation ensures institutional strategies remain dynamically aligned with pedagogical realities and societal transformations.
### 3.5 Evaluation Scenarios: From Score Evaluation to Metacognitive Diagnosis
Traditional educational evaluation systems exhibit systemic limitations rooted in two structural deficiencies. First, their rote memorization is exemplified by closed-book testing in disciplines such as language and chemistry. This traditional paradigm prioritizes knowledge point reproduction over higher-order competencies, fostering a high-score, but low-competency paradox where evaluational outcomes are misaligned with practical skill demands in fields such as computer science and medicine (Pan [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0031)). Although AI-powered evaluation systems offer transformative potential through automated large-scale data processing and predictive modeling of latent learning factors, current implementations face critical challenges. Technical limitations include insufficient integration of AI capabilities with pedagogical contexts, such as failure to correlate programming syntax errors with collaborative problem-solving behaviors, and unresolved ethical risks in data governance (J. Zhou et al. [2023](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0049)). Addressing these gaps requires systematic optimization of multimodal data collection and privacy-preserving analytics architectures to enable truly personalized competency assessment.
#### 3.5.1 Improving the Comprehensibility of the Problem-Solving Process
Students' metacognitive abilities are the basis for the optimization of adaptive propositions. Metacognitive abilities include monitoring, planning, and evaluating their own learning processes, which can help students better understand their learning status and needs (Díaz et al. [2023](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0009)). For example, DeepSeek can be used to generate a cognitive strategy diagnostic report to locate students' metacognitive weaknesses and recommend targeted training programs. The mathematical training in DeepSeek also improves the model's performance on general tasks, such as multitask language understanding and complex reasoning, enabling it to interpret the results across a wide range of tasks and to explain the solution process of interdisciplinary problems (Shao et al. [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0035)).
#### 3.5.2 Resource-Efficient Scalability With Enhanced Privacy Protections
DeepSeek's innovations in operational efficiency and localized deployment address two critical challenges in modern educational assessment systems, including equitable resource allocation and student data security. Compared to conventional approaches, DeepSeek-V2 reduces operational costs while achieving faster processing speeds, enabling reliable performance in large-scale testing scenarios (DeepSeek-Al et al. [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0007)). Crucially, its localized deployment model ensures sensitive student data remain securely stored within institutional servers, eliminating risks associated with cloud data transmission. This architecture allows schools to comply with stringent privacy regulations while maintaining full governance over data access permissions. By minimizing reliance on external computing infrastructure, DeepSeek demonstrates scalable, cost-effective solutions that prioritize both operational efficiency and ethical data stewardship. One school's attempt to introduce an expression-recognition system immediately sparked widespread controversy among parents, who feared that the technology could invade their students' psychological privacy or even turn into a form of undetected psychological surveillance, and advocated for the development of lightweight endpoint solutions, such as an offline version of DeepSeek-Lite (Z. Li [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0022)).
#### 3.5.3 Multimodal Adaptive Assessment Systems
The evolution of large-scale digital assessments necessitates intelligent systems capable of addressing both academic proficiency and learner well-being. DeepSeek's multimodal adaptive assessment framework bridges this gap by integrating behavioral, cognitive, and affective data into a responsive educational ecosystem. During assessments, the system synthesizes diverse interaction patterns, such as writing intensity, visual attention shifts, and verbal response fluency, to generate holistic learner profiles. This approach enables real-time instructional adjustments, where the system dynamically prioritizes underdeveloped competencies while maintaining alignment with curriculum objectives. For instance, students struggling with abstract problem-solving in physics receive targeted scaffolding activities that break down complex concepts into incremental challenges, reducing cognitive overload. Crucially, the system incorporates affective computing principles to mitigate stress by interspersing diagnostic tasks and adaptive feedback pacing (Bell [2023](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0003)). This paradigm shifts assessment from static knowledge measurement to developmental mentoring, fostering resilience alongside academic growth through continuous, personalized partnerships between students and teachers. In addition, DeepSeek-R1 enables cross-language support and excels in algebra and structured problems when solving Serbian high school math competition problems (Vučićević et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0037)).
In total, DeepSeek's transition from score-centric evaluation to developmental mentoring exemplifies assessment's evolution into a formative growth catalyst. By fusing metacognitive insights with empathetic computational design, the system nurtures resilient, self-regulated learners prepared for unpredictable real-world challenges.
## 4 Challenges and Future Prospects
### 4.1 Challenges of DeepSeek in Education
#### 4.1.1 Limitations of DeepSeek's Deep Thinking
DeepSeek employs chain-of-thought (CoT) methodology to enhance analytical reasoning capabilities, during pretraining constructing annotated datasets that contain CoT sequences, where a question is followed by step-by-step reasoning and then an answer to guide logical reasoning generation. During inference, the model autonomously generates intermediate reasoning steps before finalizing solutions.
However, the transparent reasoning inherent in CoT may induce overreliance on predefined procedural templates among learners, potentially diminishing metacognitive engagement in self-monitoring and strategic adjustment of cognitive processes. Effective metacognition requires active evaluation of problem-solving approaches, such as assessing the efficacy of alternative strategies (Sandhaus et al. [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0033)). DeepSeek-R1's linear CoT framework presents dual implications for higher-order thinking development. Although systematic decomposition aids complex problem-solving (Z. Zhang et al. [2022](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0048)), excessive dependence on structured pathways may constrain creative ideation and nonlinear reasoning essential for innovation. For instance, critical thinking necessitates evidence evaluation and assumption interrogation (Petress [2004](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0032)), yet CoT's outputs might reduce students' inclination to scrutinize logical inconsistencies within reasoning chains.
Moreover, the problem of hallucination inherent in LLMs is not adequately addressed in DeepSeek-R1. In addition to the occasional errors in the model, it also actively fictionalizes evidence and background material in the generation process in pursuit of logical coherence and representational integrity, thereby presenting the generated content as evidential deception.
This kind of logical pretense is very likely to mislead educational users, especially students, a group with insufficient knowledge reserves and critical skills to be developed. If the model simply answers the wrong questions or provides incorrect facts, it can be recognized and corrected by human teachers or learners through their own experience. When models fabricate academic literature, make up historical events, or fictionalize scientific data to support a point of view, their output not only conveys incorrect knowledge but also inadvertently teaches a dishonest form of academic expression. As Huang et al. ([2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0014)) point out, LLMs may hallucinate during the generation process, generating seemingly reasonable, highly persuasive, and human-like feedback but actually untrue content, which is extremely harmful yet difficult to recognize, posing a significant challenge to their reliability in real-world applications and a direct threat to educational ethics, especially in scenarios that require rigorous argumentation. This poses a direct threat to educational ethics, especially in scenarios that require rigorous argumentation and authenticity, such as essay writing, analysis of scientific experiments, or reasoning in medical and legal cases, where the consequences of misrepresentation are even more serious.
In educational contexts, students risk internalizing systematic cognitive biases when perceiving CoT-structured outputs as authoritative. Such uncritical acceptance may engender trust crises when discrepancies emerge between model outputs and established knowledge, potentially undermining confidence in both AI systems and traditional educational authorities.
#### 4.1.2 Limitations of DeepSeek's Lightweight Deployment
Although DeepSeek-R1's lightweight architecture reduces technical implementation barriers, its educational adoption introduces systemic risks requiring critical examination. The growing trend of private model deployment among governmental and enterprise entities prioritizes data security and operational efficiency through interpretable reasoning systems. However, field observations reveal insufficient demand analysis and assessments preceding implementation decisions, leading to redundant infrastructure investments and resource misallocation (M. Li et al. [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0021)). Market analyses reveal opportunistic practices, where vendors repurpose obsolete hardware as premium all-in-one AI machines, exploiting institutional enthusiasm for technological modernization through unjustified pricing strategies. More fundamentally, the rapid iteration cycle of AI technologies creates sustainability challenges for current deployments. Without systematic life-cycle planning, institutions risk functional obsolescence as emerging pedagogical requirements outpace model capabilities. This shortsighted adoption pattern exacerbates structural resource mismatches, particularly in developing regions with budget constraints, ultimately hindering sustainable education informatization efforts.
On this basis, we should call on educational institutions and relevant decision-makers to look rationally at the current boom in localization deployment. They must take into account their actual needs, conduct in-depth research and technology adaptation assessment beforehand, and make scientific judgment on the timing and scale of localization deployment. Ultimately, localization deployment should be implemented strategically and purposefully.
#### 4.1.3 Challenges in DeepSeek's Multimodal Integration for Educational Applications
Multimodal fusion is a core element to drive AI toward artificial general intelligence, and from the perspective of educational application of AGI (Lee et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0020)), true multimodal capability should support the model to achieve cross-modal generalization in learning, teaching, teaching research, management and evaluation. However, DeepSeek's current multimodal framework demonstrates constrained capabilities in addressing the inherent complexity of educational ecosystems, as detailed by M. Li et al. ([2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0021)). While effectively processing isolated data streams from textual assignments, visual classroom recordings, and so on, the system struggles with dynamic cross-modal correlation essential for authentic learning environment modeling. Physics education case studies reveal critical gaps in synchronizing diagrammatic problem-solving workflows with verbal peer discussion patterns, where the model fails to establish conceptual bridges between symbolic representations and colloquial reasoning trajectories. The system's contextual interpretation limitations become pronounced when processing culturally nuanced materials, exhibiting higher misinterpretation rates compared to standardized content due to insufficient localization in training corpora (M. Li et al. [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0021)). Temporal processing constraints further compound these issues, with latency of 3–5 seconds disrupting real-time intervention capabilities during collaborative learning sessions. These operational limitations underscore the urgent need for architectural innovations in temporal synchronization mechanisms and culturally adaptive processing modules to better align with authentic educational contexts.
### 4.2 Future of DeepSeek's Application in Education
#### 4.2.1 Educational Policies and Institutional Guidance
The integration of DeepSeek into education systems necessitates policy frameworks that strategically balance technological innovation with pedagogical accountability. A primary concern lies in addressing the AI overreliance phenomena, even with the support of CoT reasoning, where learners' dependence on structured reasoning pathways may inadvertently constrain metacognitive development by reducing opportunities for self-regulated strategy evaluation (Sandhaus et al. [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0033)). To mitigate this risk, policymakers must establish AI intervention thresholds, a dynamic regulatory mechanism that adjusts the level of granularity of thought chain outputs according to the learner's stage of development. For instance, elementary mathematics curricula could permit detailed step-by-step guidance for foundational arithmetic operations, whereas advanced calculus courses might restrict CoT explanations to high-level conceptual frameworks, thereby compelling students to articulate intermediate reasoning steps independently (Z. Zhang et al. [2022](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0048)). Such thresholds would operationalize Vygotsky's zone of proximal development in AI-augmented learning, ensuring scaffolding mechanisms adaptively recede as competency matures.
Concurrently, the implementation of AIED sustainability standards is critical to counteracting systemic risks associated with lightweight deployment models. As noted by M. Li et al. ([2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0021)), the rapid iteration cycles of AI technologies and hasty institutional adoption often result in resource misallocation, particularly in regions lacking infrastructure readiness. Regulatory bodies must mandate architecture designs that decouple hardware upgrades from core pedagogical functionalities, enabling schools to maintain operational continuity across hardware generations. For developing regions, a phased upgrade plan could prioritize basic capabilities, such as literacy tutoring modules, while deferring advanced features such as multimodal integration until the localized digital ecosystem has matured.
To address equity concerns, policymakers should establish algorithmic accountability frameworks requiring transparency in AI training data composition and decision-making processes. Such frameworks would mandate third-party audits of educational AI systems to detect and rectify cultural or socioeconomic biases in content generation, ensuring culturally responsive pedagogy. It is crucial to combine these measures with international cooperation on open-source educational knowledge bases to organize the validated multidisciplinary concepts. Ultimately, the goal is to cultivate an institutional ecosystem where DeepSeek's capabilities enhance rather than dictate educational practices, ensuring human educators remain the arbiters of curricular and ethical decision-making.
#### 4.2.2 Shift of Educational Stakeholders' Roles
DeepSeek's integration necessitates redefining roles within the educational ecosystem to address its reasoning limitations. Teachers must evolve into critical AI mediators, equipped to deconstruct CoT-generated solutions and expose potential logical inconsistencies or hallucinations. Professional development programs should emphasize training educators in algorithmic critique pedagogy, enabling them to guide students in interrogating AI outputs. For example, physics instructors might contrast DeepSeek's problem-solving trajectories with alternative approaches from Nobel laureates' historical notebooks, fostering awareness of reasoning diversity.
Students require cultivation as autonomous sense-makers rather than passive consumers of AI-generated content. Curriculum reforms must integrate cognitive disengagement exercises that periodically prohibit AI use, compelling learners to articulate their problem-solving rationale without algorithmic scaffolding. Research has shown that alternating between AI-assisted and self-directed learning phases enhances error detection, a key skill in an era where AI output results are probabilistic.
Simultaneously, parental engagement frameworks should address anxieties about AI dependency through community workshops demonstrating how controlled AI interaction enhances, rather than replaces, human intellect. Parental roles require similar transformation. AI literacy workshops could demystify DeepSeek's capabilities and limitations, empowering families to navigate AI-augmented homework environments. This tripartite reconfiguration positions AI not as an authority but as a collaborative provocation, where human agency remains central to meaning-making.
#### 4.2.3 Further Technological Breakthroughs
#### 4.2.3.1 Reasoning and Generation Based on Credible Evidence and Logic
In order to mitigate the illusion problem and enhance the credibility of generated content, future model generation must move toward external validation and structured reasoning. At the factual level, the core strategy is to adopt the retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) paradigm. This paradigm requires the model to compulsorily retrieve evidence from a trusted knowledge base before answering. As pointed out in G. Zhang et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0044) and H. Zhang et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0045), RAG is a core strategy to enhance the factuality of models and reduce illusions by introducing an authoritative knowledge base. The model will be transformed into an organizer and explicator of trustworthy knowledge, and each factual statement it generates can be accompanied by a traceable citation of the source. Béchard and Ayala's ([2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0001)) study further confirms that the implementation of RAG not only significantly reduces illusions in structured outputs but also improves the model's ability to deal with generalization to problems out of domains and that an efficient retriever reduces the need for a large model even in terms of its own size dependence.
At the logical level, knowledge graphs are needed to provide the skeleton of reasoning. Research has shown that combining knowledge graphs with large models can provide structured facts to fill in knowledge gaps, thereby effectively suppressing illusory content that may appear plausible but is factually incorrect (Lavrinovics et al. [2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0019)). More research, such as the chain-of-knowledge (CoK) framework, has explored how models can mimic the human knowledge exploration process by learning the knowledge graph, thus significantly improving their reasoning ability (Y. Zhang et al. [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0047)).
Ultimately, an RAG + KG paradigm could be constructed in the future. When a complex question is posed, the model first retrieves relevant text and data fragments from authoritative repositories. The retrieved information entities are localized in the knowledge graph, and a logical relationship path between them is found. Along this path, which has both credible evidence and knowledge logic, the answer is generated, and its cited sources and reasoning nodes are displayed throughout.
#### 4.2.3.2 The Collaborative Intelligence and Distributed Deployment
Advancing DeepSeek's educational utility requires innovations spanning multiagent systems and embodied AI frameworks. The emergence of educational multiple agent systems introduces collaborative problem-solving paradigms. In chemistry labs, a teacher agent orchestrates specialized individual agents, such as reaction predictor, safety auditor, and hypothesis generator, to simulate experimentation guided by experts. Multiagent reinforcement learning allows these systems to optimize pedagogical strategies in real time.
End-to-end embodied AI frameworks further bridge digital and physical learning. DeepSeek's integration with robotics platforms enables hands-on STEM activities, where AI tutors can analyze sensor data from students' prototypes. It is important to leverage multimodal fusion, which synchronizes speech, gesture, and environmental data, to mimic human tutors' situational awareness.
The edge-to-cloud adaptive model ecosystem is the key to sustainable deployment at scale, and the DeepSeek family derives a series of models with different model sizes, capabilities, focuses, and deployment environments. The DeepSeek family derives from a base model through knowledge distillation to form a vertically integrated models as a model family that vary in model size, capabilities, and deployment environments.
The DeepSeek's family includes lightweight, even nano models that have been minimally compressed at the edge. They are embedded directly into students' tablets, teachers' smartphones, classroom smart devices, and even IoT sensors, and are designed for scenarios that require immediate response, offline operation, and absolute data security. For example, advanced compression techniques, such as differentiable neural architecture search (DARTS), can reduce the model size while still retaining accuracy on the core task, which makes it possible to run localized DeepSeek instances on Raspberry Pi clusters even in remote schools with limited resources (M. Li et al. [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0021)).
A fully functional and powerful basic large model that supports complex multimodal analysis and generation is deployed as a cloud counterpart in the form of a cluster of servers, providing a source of higher-order cognitive support and model updates at the edge.
Ultimately, through federated learning and other privacy protection protocols, these models deployed at the edge can continue to refine knowledge and iterate capabilities from the cloud global model, and together build a self-evolving, continuous distributed education AI ecosystem.
#### 4.2.3.3 Multimodal Information Fusion Analysis
Dual coding theory posits that humans possess two cognitive subsystems, including a verbal system, which processes linguistic information through mediums such as verbal text, and an episodic system, which is responsible for processing nonverbal information such as image perception (Paivio [1979](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0030)). These two systems are independent of each other, but they also interact synergistically to enhance memory and meaning construction. Whether mimicking human cognition to enhance multimodal comprehension or adapting to human cognition for generation, future multimodal models will need to deeply integrate multimodal information to better integrate into human educational contexts and provide rich services and support. Current research on multimodal information fusion includes word embedding in the early stage of processing in the grand language model, feature extraction in the middle stage, integration of results in the late stage, and hybrid strategies combining multiple fusion approaches mentioned above (J. Wang et al. [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0039)). Alternatively, the core architecture of the large language model, based on the attention mechanism, will be able to compute weights across modalities. When the model reads a text, its attention can be focused on specific regions of the image and vice versa.
In addition, current research also takes into account the fact that the traditional knowledge graphs described in the previous section are mainly constructed based on text, which makes it difficult to utilize multimodal information such as images and audio and fails to adequately simulate the multimodal characteristics of human cognitive systems. After embedding multimodal information and graph structure into a unified space (Liang et al. [2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/fer3.70022#fer370022-bib-0023)), the relationship between the nodes of multimodal information not only assists in reasoning, but also improves the contextual comprehension of the LLMs during the reasoning and thinking process of the large model.
## 5 Conclusion
DeepSeek's AI-driven technologies have the potential to influence educational practice by providing scalable, adaptive, and multimodal tools. Its core innovations, enhanced by reasoning efficiency, lightweight deployment, and cross-modal fusion, enable personalized learning, real-time teacher support, and metacognitive diagnostics, transcending the limitations of traditional and ordinary AI models. However, the transformative potential is tempered by challenges such as hallucinatory outputs, rigid reasoning patterns, and deployment risks in resource-constrained environments. Future advancements must prioritize hybrid architectures for multimodal integration, context-aware intent recognition, and ethical frameworks to ensure transparency and accountability. By balancing technological innovation with responsible practices, DeepSeek can foster equitable access to intelligent education, empower educators, and nurture higher-order thinking in learners. As AI continues to evolve, DeepSeek's journey underscores the imperative of aligning technical progress with pedagogical and ethical imperatives, ultimately shaping a future where AI serves as a collaborative partner in human-centric education.
