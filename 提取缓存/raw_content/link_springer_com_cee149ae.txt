Abstract
--------

The incorporation of artificial intelligence (AI) in education has gained substantial attention due to its numerous advantages. However, existing studies rarely investigate the application of AI technologies in K-12 schools, particularly in Asia. This study seeks to analyze research trends through bibliometric analysis, tracing the evolution of AI in K-12 education (AIEdK-12) across Asian countries from 1996 to 2025. A total of 531 articles were retrieved from the Scopus database for analysis. Descriptive bibliographic data was processed using Microsoft Excel and Bibliometrix, while network visualization was conducted through VOSviewer. The results reveal a growing interest in AI applications in K-12 education within Asia over the past 30 years. China stands out with the highest volume of publications, while Hong Kong leads in terms of citation counts. The Chinese University of Hong Kong was identified as the most active institution, contributing 60 publications. Education and Information Technologies, the leading journal in the field, published 27 articles and accumulated 442 citations. The most cited article, authored by Hwang et al., received 174 citations. Notably, T.K.F. Chiu from The Chinese University of Hong Kong authored 16 papers and holds an h-index of 14. Keyword analysis revealed that “artificial intelligence,” “machine learning,” “AI education,” “deep learning,” and “chatbot” are among the most frequently used terms, highlighting the primary research themes in this area. This study provides valuable insights into the current landscape of AIEdK-12 in Asia, outlining significant research areas and offering guidance for future investigations.

1 Introduction
--------------

Artificial intelligence (AI) has become increasingly vital in the education sector and continues to gain traction, especially following the emergence of ChatGPT [[1](#ref-CR1),[2](#ref-CR2),[3](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR3)]. In recent years, AI has captured the interest of educators and researchers who are exploring various learner-centered approaches to integrating AI technology into classrooms. This growing interest stems from AI’s potential to enhance curricula and support the learning process [[4](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR4), [5](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR5)]. Research on AI in education, including chatbots, intelligent tutoring systems, virtual learning environments, and grading systems, has advanced considerably over the past decade [[6](#ref-CR6),[7](#ref-CR7),[8](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR8)]. While there is no universally accepted definition of AI, Díaz and Nussbaum [[9](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR9)] offer a more contemporary perspective, conceptualizing AI as an educational technology capable of identifying patterns in existing or real-time data and autonomously making instructional decisions designed or implemented for pedagogical purposes to improve teaching and learning. Consequently, AI in education (AIEd) broadly refers to the application of AI technologies, such as machine learning and deep learning, to enhance the effectiveness of teaching and learning processes across school subjects.

Over the past decade, scholars worldwide have identified numerous advantages of AIEd. As highlighted in earlier studies, AI has been recognized for its ability to support personalized learning, provide adaptive feedback, automate various teaching processes, and tailor instruction to students’ specific needs [[10](#ref-CR10),[11](#ref-CR11),[12](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR12)]. These benefits, which are not typically found in traditional education settings, not only enhance the efficiency and quality of education but also offer students a more comfortable and individualized learning experience [[13](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR13)]. Literature suggests that AI technology is becoming increasingly important for student learning and development in the twenty-first century. With AI, teachers can explore the most effective teaching strategies to create innovative and personalized learning experiences [[14](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR14)], ultimately fostering greater student engagement in the learning process [[4](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR4)]. In this context, students not only receive learning experiences tailored to their unique preferences but also gain personalized, real-time feedback on their academic performance [[2](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR2)]. Therefore, AI is seen as having the potential to revolutionize education in general, with a particularly significant impact on K-12 educational settings.

Nowadays, AI has significantly transformed K-12 schooling, shifting traditional classroom settings into AI-powered learning environments. As technology continues to advance, the integration of AI in K-12 education (AIEdK-12) holds great potential to revolutionize the way school subjects are taught while also fostering students’ interest in AI from an early age [[15](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR15)]. In this context, K-12 education (from kindergarten to grade 12) plays a crucial role in providing students with the essential skills and knowledge needed to navigate an AI-driven world. Beyond serving as a foundation for preparing the next generation to be digitally proficient in AI-related competencies and knowledge, thereby enhancing their readiness for future careers [[16](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR16), [17](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR17)], K-12 education also anticipates the social and technological transformations that AI is expected to bring [[18](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR18), [19](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR19)]. This underscores the necessity of integrating AI education into K-12 learning environments to better equip students for a society increasingly shaped by AI [[20](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR20)], highlighting the growing significance of AI applications in K-12 environments.

Many existing studies have explored the effectiveness of AIEdK-12 from multiple perspectives, including attitudes toward AI [[21](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR21)], AI literacy [[17](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR17)], computational thinking [[22](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR22)], creative problem-solving [[21](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR21)], academic achievement [[23](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR23)], and motivation [[24](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR24)]. For instance, Ferrarelli and Iocchi [[25](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR25)] introduced an educational project that enables students to design, implement, and visualize physics concepts through programming a mobile robot. Their study demonstrated that engaging high school students in programming a mobile robot for physics experiments enhances their understanding of Newtonian physics. Similarly, Figueiredo et al. [[26](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR26)] found that AI plays a significant role in improving both comprehension of chemistry concepts and motivation among students in grades 10 through 12. In addition, Deveci Topal et al. [[27](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR27)] examined the influence of AI-powered chatbots in a 5th-grade science class and reported that chatbot applications had a notable impact on student achievement. Moreover, students generally found these activities enjoyable and stimulating, fostering their curiosity about science. Lastly, Chen et al. [[28](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR28)] investigated the effect of a newly developed chatbot on learning Chinese vocabulary, reporting a substantial improvement in students’ learning performance. These findings indicate that AI has the potential to contribute significantly to both teaching and learning. Consequently, reviewing and synthesizing existing literature on AIEdK-12 is essential to consolidate current knowledge and inspire future research and educational practices. Therefore, a comprehensive review of the latest studies on AIEdK-12 is necessary.

As empirical studies on the use of AI in educational settings continue to emerge, many literature reviews, including bibliometric analyses, have been conducted [[5](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR5), [11](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR11), [14](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR14), [29](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR29)]. However, these reviews primarily focus on higher education, with little specific attention given to K-12 classrooms. In fact, most other review studies cover all levels of education rather than being exclusively dedicated to K-12 contexts [[2](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR2), [6](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR6), [12](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR12), [13](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR13)]. Despite the growing body of review studies on AI integration in education in recent years, a noticeable gap remains in the context of K-12 education. This is particularly significant as AI curricula, cognitive development levels, and teaching and learning practices in K-12 settings differ substantially from those in higher education [[16](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR16)]. The lack of targeted reviews on AI applications in kindergarten through twelfth grade highlights a critical gap in the literature [[12](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR12)], emphasizing the need for more specialized studies in this domain.

Asia, home to approximately 60% of the global population, has been established as a formidable region due to its vast potential for AI applications. Numerous Asian nations have made substantial investments in AI development initiatives [[8](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR8), [18](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR18)]. This underscores the significance of examining research patterns and trends related to AIEdK-12 across Asia. The Asian context was chosen for several reasons. First, Asia has been rapidly advancing in science, technology, and education. Over the past decade, many countries in the region have undertaken educational reforms by aligning their curricula with global standards and fostering innovation [[30](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR30)]. Additionally, given the strong presence of Asian countries in the technology sector, AI has emerged as a key area of focus in research, teaching methodologies, and policy development [[18](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR18)]. Analyzing research trends in AIEdK-12 within Asia enables scholars to gain deeper insights into how different countries in the region are engaging with this field. Second, there is a growing need for comparative studies on AIEdK-12, particularly within this region. By exploring research developments in AI for K-12 settings in Asia, researchers can gain valuable perspectives on AI implementation in various countries. This research also offers clarity regarding the progression and transformation of AIEdK-12, shedding light on the future directions of studies in this area.

Given the increasing number of empirical studies examining the use of AI in K-12 classrooms across Asian countries, a bibliometric analysis is essential. In this study, we selected bibliometric analysis as the most suitable method to bridge the aforementioned research gap and to conduct both quantitative and qualitative investigations into AI-related publications in K-12 school settings within Asia. This approach provides a comprehensive overview of the primary research interests in this field. The methodology employed in the current study offers researchers insights into the patterns and trends in scholarly publications on AIEdK-12 originating from Asia. Additionally, bibliometric analysis aids researchers in identifying the most influential countries, institutions, journals, authors, and publications in this domain [[31](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR31)]. Reviewing publication trends in AIEdK-12 research conducted in Asia will not only shed light on past and present developments but also inspire future researchers in the region to explore new possibilities and opportunities for advancing studies in this area while influencing teaching and learning practices. This study serves as a valuable reference for future researchers interested in AIEdK-12 and provides an up-to-date summary of the research literature, enhancing understanding in this field.

Nowadays, the significance of AI education is growing rapidly, and research exploring the impact of AI on primary and secondary education in Asia is expected to continue expanding. To support scientific inquiry into AIEdK-12, numerous recent bibliometric studies have been conducted to map research performance in this area [[4](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR4), [9](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR9), [16](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR16), [19](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR19), [20](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR20)]. However, no comprehensive bibliometric analysis has yet been undertaken to explore AI applications in K-12 learning contexts across Asia. Although several bibliometric studies have attempted to investigate AIEdK-12, their focus has generally been delimited to a single country, a limited dataset, or a specific academic field. For instance, Lee and Kwon [[8](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR8)] conducted a meta-analysis on the effectiveness of AI education in K-12 classrooms in South Korea from 2019 to 2023. Their findings indicated that AI education had a substantial impact on students’ cognitive and affective domains. Nevertheless, their study was restricted to one country and analyzed only 64 studies. Similarly, Kavitha and Joshith [[29](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR29)] performed a bibliometric analysis and systematic review of the integration of AI technologies in K-12 science education between 2013 and 2023. They examined 20 journal articles and conference proceedings, revealing that AI technologies contributed to improved learning outcomes, heightened student engagement, and facilitated personalized learning in science education. In a similar study, Heeg and Avraamidou [[10](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR10)] carried out a systematic literature review on AI applications in school science, analyzing 22 studies published between 2010 and 2021. Their findings suggested that most AI applications were concentrated in geoscience and physics and were primarily used to support either knowledge construction or skill development. However, both of these studies were limited to the domain of science education and only included articles published up to 2023. This highlights that while several reviews on AI in education have been conducted in the past two to three years, they may not fully capture more recent developments. Additionally, Yang et al. [[32](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR32)] performed a bibliometric analysis of 210 studies on AI-enhanced special education in K-12 settings between 2013 and 2023. Their study identified emerging trends focusing on mathematics learning outcomes and special educational needs. However, the analysis was based on a relatively small sample size. Overall, existing review studies have examined limited timeframes, potentially affecting the generalizability of their conclusions due to the restricted number of publications included.

Unlike previous studies that focused on relatively short time frames, the current study offers a more extensive analysis of AIEdK-12 over three decades. The goal is to gain a more up-to-date and relevant understanding of how AIEdK-12 is utilized in Asian countries. To date, no review has covered a time frame extending to 2025. Given the continuous integration of AI into K-12 learning environments by educators and researchers [[4](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR4), [8](#ref-CR8),[9](#ref-CR9),[10](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR10), [15](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR15), [16](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR16), [19](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR19), [20](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR20), [29](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR29)] and the steady accumulation of research data, a more targeted and in-depth investigation of AIEdK-12 across Asia is essential. To advance scholarly inquiry, this study takes a distinct approach by evaluating the performance of AIEdK-12 research up to January 2025 within Asia. The analysis draws from all education-related journals indexed in the Scopus database. Examining this topic provides insights into the readership scope of academic publications authored by researchers affiliated with Asian institutions and allows for a comparative assessment of each country’s research standing within the region. Future scholars may gain valuable insights into the present landscape of AI-related studies within K-12 education across Asia. Therefore, this paper aims to conduct a bibliometric analysis of AI-related publications in K-12 contexts by Asian researchers, addressing gaps in existing literature. The research questions guiding this study are as follows:

1. 1.
   
   How has the distribution of AIEdK-12 publications in Asia evolved from inception in 1996 to early 2025?
2. 2.
   
   Which Asian countries, institutions, and journals have contributed the most to AI research in K-12 education?
3. 3.
   
   Which publications have garnered the highest number of citations, and who are the most influential authors in this field?
4. 4.
   
   What are the most frequently explored topics or concepts, as indicated by the author’s keywords in these publications?

2 Methodology
-------------

### 2.1 Study design

To expand the understanding of AIEdK-12, the current study employed bibliometric analysis to explore prevailing trends, recent advancements, and emerging themes over the past three decades. By examining a wide time frame, this study provides an in-depth overview of the historical evolution of the field and how research topics have shifted over time. Through bibliometric analysis, the study aims to systematically map the scientific knowledge base with a more focused approach while highlighting long-term trends and research developments related to AIEdK-12 in the Asian region. This method enables researchers to identify scholarly contributions, analyze relational patterns, track the growth of publications and citations over the years, and examine topic structures and research trends [[33](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR33)]. Consequently, bibliometric analysis was deemed an appropriate method to address the research questions.

### 2.2 Data collection

An extensive online search was conducted utilizing the Scopus database to collect articles published up to 2025. The data collection took place on January 31, 2025. In order to avoid inconsistencies in the volume of data due to potential database updates, all searches and downloads were performed on the same day. We chose the Scopus for the following reasons. Scopus was selected over other databases, such as the Web of Science, due to its broader coverage of educational sources [[34](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR34)]. As highlighted by Pizzi et al. [[35](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR35)], Scopus is widely recognized for bibliometric analysis because it reduces the risks of bias and omissions that may arise from relying on a limited range of journals. Moreover, Scopus is acknowledged as one of the most extensive bibliographic databases worldwide, allowing for efficient access to bibliographic data necessary for bibliometric software [[36](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR36)]. Elsevier [[37](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR37)] reported that Scopus indexes over 26,000 peer-reviewed journals, more than 1100 book series, and over 11.7 million conference papers spanning all scientific disciplines. In addition, Donthu et al. [[31](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR31)] noted that the advent of scientific databases, such as Scopus, has significantly simplified the acquisition of large-scale bibliometric data, enabling systematic data analysis and fostering greater academic interest in bibliometric studies. Consequently, this study relied on Scopus as the primary source for data collection.

This research employed the PRISMA framework to systematically analyze data and maintain the quality of the article selection process [[38](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR38)]. The method consists of four key phases: identification, screening, eligibility assessment, and final inclusion. Several criteria were established to guide the selection and evaluation of publications from Scopus. To conduct a thorough literature review, we developed a search strategy aimed at identifying scholarly works related to both AI and K-12 education within the context of Asian countries (see Table [1](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#Tab1)). The search query was formulated to capture studies referencing terms such as artificial intelligence, machine learning, machine intelligence, and other related terms, in combination with K-12 education. For a study to be considered, both topics had to be present in the title, abstract, or keywords. The selection of these specific terms was informed by prior literature [[9](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR9), [12](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR12), [16](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR16), [19](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR19), [32](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR32)], which identified them as the most pertinent to the discourse on AI in K-12 education. These terms are commonly co-occurring due to their conceptual interrelations. To ensure the relevance and academic rigor of the included studies, we manually reviewed the titles, abstracts, and keywords of all retrieved articles, verifying that the terms appeared within an appropriate context. This approach ensured that only relevant studies were included in the final review.

To ensure the relevance and quality of the documents, specific inclusion and exclusion criteria (Table [2](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#Tab2)) were performed. Initially, 4388 documents were yielded. After filtering out studies that did not originate from Asian countries, the dataset was reduced to 1888 articles. Further refinement was applied by selecting only documents classified as “Article” and in the “Final” stage, narrowing the selection to 858 articles. An additional criterion was applied to include only English-language publications, resulting in a dataset of 812 articles. All documents retrieved through the search process were then manually reviewed to eliminate lexical ambiguities and to assess whether the titles, abstracts, and keywords included terms related to both AI and K-12 education. Following this rigorous screening, 531 publications were ultimately deemed relevant and included in this review. The earliest article in the dataset was published in 1996 [[40](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR40)]. ### 2.3 Inclusion and exclusion criteria

The inclusion and exclusion criteria are presented in Table [2](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#Tab2). The inclusion criteria focused on selecting articles authored by Asian researchers and published in scientific journals between January 1996 and January 2025 (data retrieval time). It should be noted that the search was conducted without restrictions on publication years, enabling a comprehensive review of recent developments. The selection of the period starting from 1996 is based on the fact that it marks the publication of the first known article related to AIEdK-12 within the context of Asian countries, as identified in our bibliometric dataset. This year serves as a logical starting point to trace the chronological development, research patterns, and thematic evolution of AIEdK-12 over time.

The bibliometric analysis was limited to journal articles written in English, as such publications undergo a rigorous peer-review process and are regarded as reliable sources of scientific knowledge. English-language articles were chosen due to the language’s predominant role in global scientific communication. Meanwhile, the exclusion criteria included documents written in languages other than English, not affiliated with countries and territories in Asia, and classified as non-journal articles. This methodological approach ensures that the bibliometric study focuses exclusively on findings that are both scientifically validated and highly credible. Thus, this study offers important perspectives on publication trends in AIEdK-12 research originating from Asia or involving at least one author affiliated with an institution based in the region.

### 2.4 Data analysis

The selected article sample was retrieved from the Scopus database in a comma-separated values (CSV) format. Once the sample was converted into CSV, a bibliometric analysis was performed utilizing the R package Bibliometrix [[41](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR41)] and VOSviewer [[42](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR42)] to obtain in-depth bibliometric insights. This study established various bibliometric parameters, including the publication trends, identification of key emerging keywords over the past three decades, examination of AIEdK-12 journals, assessment of the most productive authors, institutions, and countries, analysis of the most frequently cited papers in the field of AIEdK-12 within the Asian context.

This study utilized two analytical methods: performance analysis and science mapping. The performance analysis involved a descriptive review of all retrieved publications, including the annual publication count, publication sources, contributing authors, countries or regions, and affiliated institutions. This analysis was conducted using Bibliometrix and Microsoft Excel. Meanwhile, science mapping was carried out using VOSviewer to generate visual representations of connections among countries, authors, and keywords, along with their interrelations [[42](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR42)]. This tool facilitated co-authorship analysis and keyword co-occurrence analysis within the AIEdK-12 research domain. Specifically, co-authorship analysis was employed to explore scholarly interactions and collaborations within Asia. To identify the main themes discussed in the publications, a co-occurrence analysis of author keywords was performed. Co-word analysis operates on the premise that frequently co-occurring keywords indicate a thematic connection between the associated topics. Author keywords, which are terms selected by the authors to represent the content of their research, serve as a valuable instrument for understanding the knowledge structure of a scientific discipline. The size of each node reflects the occurrence frequency, while the curves connecting the nodes represent their co-occurrence within the same publication. A shorter distance between two nodes signifies a higher frequency of co-occurrence between the corresponding keywords.

In bibliometric analysis, two key weighting attributes are frequently employed to describe an item: the links attribute and the total link strength (TLS) attribute. The links attribute indicates how many connections an item has with other items, while the TLS attribute reflects the combined strength of these connections. For example, in the case of co-authorship networks among countries, the links attribute denotes the total number of co-authorship ties a particular country has with others. Meanwhile, the TLS attribute captures the overall magnitude of these collaborations. These attributes were determined using VOSviewer, utilizing the full counting method.

3 Results and discussion
------------------------

AI technology has been integrated into K-12 curricula in various ways, leading to a significant rise in research on the use of AI tools and applications in AIEdK-12. As a result, studies in this area have surged since 2019, with the majority of publications emerging within the past five years. Conducting a review on AIEd, Ekin et al. [[6](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR6)] reached a similar conclusion, highlighting the growing academic interest in AI’s role in education. One possible explanation for this trend is that advancements in AI technology have fueled the rapid expansion of AI applications in K-12 schooling. The emergence of AI chatbots, digital textbooks powered by AI, AI-based learning platforms, AI-supported assessments, and machine learning offers substantial benefits for both online and in-person learning environments [[7](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR7), [45](#ref-CR45),[46](#ref-CR46),[47](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR47)]. Furthermore, the pandemic accelerated the adoption of AI technologies in education, further contributing to the rise in research publications on this topic [[1](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR1), [12](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR12)].

It is important to acknowledge that the advancement of AI has primarily been utilized to enhance teaching quality and support administrative tasks [[6](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR6), [12](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR12)]. For instance, the emergence of machine learning, intelligent tutors, large language models, and ChatGPT has transformed how students learn and teachers instruct, revolutionizing conventional teaching methods [[2](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR2), [7](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR7), [14](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR14), [29](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR29)]. AI is gaining prominence in K-12 schools due to its ability to automate task assessments [[48](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR48)], provide personalized learning experiences [[11](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR11)], deliver instant student feedback [[2](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR2)], adapt teaching strategies to individual student needs [[13](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR13)], and create more engaging and immersive learning environments [[49](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR49)]. It is known that the support AI offers enables students to become more engaged, motivated, and independent in their learning process [[4](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR4)]. Given these benefits, it is crucial to equip K-12 students with AI knowledge and literacy from an early age. AI can enhance both learning environments and student experiences, providing significant benefits in K-12 education. Incorporating AI into the K-12 curriculum is a vital strategic initiative in preparing future generations [[50](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR50)]. Additionally, automated assessment facilitated by AI allows teachers and administrators to streamline their administrative responsibilities more efficiently [[6](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR6), [12](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR12)]. It is also noted that AI provides educators with intelligent teaching tools, such as virtual reality, to enrich instructional content and pedagogical approaches. Consequently, AI is increasingly recognized as a promising pedagogical innovation at the K-12 level.

Currently, an increasing number of researchers are exploring the use of AI applications such as automated grading, intelligent tutoring systems, virtual reality, and recommender systems [[48](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR48), [49](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR49)] in K-12 teaching and learning. The impact of AI on students’ cognitive and affective development has also been widely reported. For example, AI has been shown to enhance student engagement and interest [[49](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR49)], facilitate learning flow and improve STEAM literacy [[51](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR51)], improve core mathematical skills and problem-solving abilities [[52](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR52)], develop computational thinking skills [[22](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR22)], promote AI literacy [[17](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR17)], reduce reading anxiety [[46](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR46)], predict student dropouts [[53](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR53)], enrich learning experiences [[27](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR27)], encourage scientific argumentation [[48](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR48)], and enhance academic performance [[25](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR25), [49](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR49), [52](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR52)]. By leveraging AI technology, teachers can accurately predict students’ academic performance and suggest learning resources tailored to their preferences [[7](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR7), [13](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR13)]. Consequently, AI in education is considered more effective than traditional teaching methods. It is proven that through AI algorithms and data analysis, student performance, habits, and learning needs can be effectively monitored while taking into account their interests and learning styles [[6](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR6), [13](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR13)]. In addition, AI enables teachers to concentrate on higher-order skills such as creativity and focus on tasks with greater value [[2](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR2), [5](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR5)].

Considering the growth rate over the past decade (0.75% of publications in 2014 and 37.29% in 2024), it is projected that the volume of research publications on AIEdK-12 will continue to increase significantly. This trend aligns with Price’s law, which reveals that scientific output tends to double within a ten-year period [[54](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR54)]. The rise in publications reflects the increasing significance of AIEdK-12 in Asia. These findings support previous research recognizing AI as a crucial and rapidly expanding field across disciplines, including education [[2](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR2), [4](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR4), [5](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR5), [13](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR13), [14](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR14)]. The substantial growth in AI-driven K-12 education publications highlights its lasting impact on the academic landscape and suggests that its influence will continue to expand. This observation is consistent with earlier studies that have underscored the transformative role of AIEd [[17](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR17), [22](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR22), [25](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR25), [27](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR27), [46](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR46), [49](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR49)]. Consequently, the findings of the present study contribute to the growing body of evidence reinforcing the critical role of AIEdK-12 research in shaping the future of inclusive educational practices.

### 3.2 Most relevant countries

Table [4](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#Tab4), according to the results from VOSviewer software, lists the top 10 out of 51 Asian countries/territories contributing to AIEdK-12 research between 1996 and January 2025, with key contributors identified based on their number of publications. It is interesting to notice that among the top 10 nations, the majority of these studies were conducted in East Asia (74.90%), indicating strong academic engagement in this area. These were followed by research efforts in Southeast Asia (9.64%), West Asia (9.04%), and South Asia (6.43%); there were no studies from Central Asia. It is also noted that Singapore was the early pioneer in AIEdK-12, with the first published studies appearing in 1996. In Singapore, Fok and Ong [[40](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR40)] initiated a high school project integrating AI in robotics, utilizing artificial neural networks to develop a Tic-Tac-Toe-playing system. Although studies from China, South Korea, and Hong Kong emerged later, they have progressively taken the lead in publication volume within this field.

China has been established as the leading contributor in this research domain, surpassing all other Asian countries with an impressive 180 publications indexed in Scopus, accounting for 33.90% of the total output. This underscores its dominant position in the field. This can be attributed to the extensive research carried out by scholars in China, who have explored various innovative applications of AI within the K-12 educational setting. From the Scopus dataset, the remaining countries/territories had publications of fewer than 15. Prominent AI industry leaders, including Google, Microsoft, and International Business Machines (IBM), have a significant presence in these two countries. This suggests that collaboration among technology companies, universities, and education researchers plays a crucial role in improving publication output and accelerating the adoption of AIEdK-12 in these regions. Moreover, the Chinese and South Korean governments have integrated AI into their K-12 education systems [[8](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR8), [18](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR18)], which has further driven scientific advancements in this area. Also, China leads in AI-related publications, likely due to its status as a large and developed country with abundant research funding opportunities for AI scholars [[4](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR4)]. Recently, Jiang [[56](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR56)] reported that China has allocated US$8.2 billion to AI development, designating it as a national priority, which has contributed to the rise in research publications in the field.

### 3.4 Most impactful journals

Table [6](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#Tab6) displays the ten most significant journals publishing research on AIEdK-12 studies from Asian countries over the years. Among the 264 journals identified in the database, the ten most prominent journals have been cited at least 186 times. A total of 531 articles were analyzed, spanning 264 different journals. When ranked by the number of citations, *Education and Information Technologies*, published by Springer and indexed in Q1, holds the highest impact in terms of both total citations (442) and number of publications (27) since its first related publication in 2021. Such dominance reflects the journal’s dedication to fostering comprehensive discussions and advancements in AIEdK-12 applications. The possible reason behind this is that EIT, established in 1996, is among the longest-running international scientific journals integrating technology and education, with a high publication frequency of 18 issues per year. The data also reveals that EIT is the most frequently referenced journal in this domain. This indicates that the journal has gained significant academic recognition. A possible reason could be that EIT is dedicated to leveraging computing technology to improve education and learning across diverse contexts, platforms, and environments. As one of the leading US-based journals, EIT, published by Springer, holds an h-index of 12, positioning it in the top quartile. The h-index serves as a measure of a journal’s research output and influence within its field, while the SJR reflects the academic prestige of a journal based on its citation impact [[14](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR14)]. These metrics suggest that publications in EIT could play a crucial role in shaping the future research trajectory of AIEdK-12 across Asia.

Larger circles denote topics that are discussed more frequently. The size of each circle corresponds to the number of publications that include the keyword, meaning that a larger node signifies more frequent use of that term. It should be noted that different colors distinguish various keyword clusters. Co-word analysis, as mentioned by Narong and Hallinger [[72](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR72)], is a widely applied method for mapping the conceptual framework of academic literature related to a specific research area.

4 Conclusions and implications
------------------------------

### 4.1 Conclusions

This study aimed to provide a comprehensive overview of AIEdK-12 research across Asia. The analysis presents a broad perspective on AI-related studies conducted in K-12 school settings within various Asian countries. Over the past 30 years, interest in AIEdK-12 has shown a steady increase in Asia, as evidenced by the growing number of publications on this subject. The findings indicate that from 1996 to 2017, the publication rate remained relatively low and stagnant, suggesting a limited number of relevant studies in the early years. However, a significant increase occurred after 2017 with a peak in 2024, demonstrating heightened interest and enthusiasm for AIEdK-12 within the academic community. It is believed that this sharp rise is likely attributed to advancements in AI technology and potential applications of AIEdK-12, as well as increasing interest and investment in AI-driven educational technologies [[10](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR10), [16](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR16), [18](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR18)]. In other words, this trend reflects the continuous progress in both technology and education across the Asian region [[8](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR8), [18](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR18), [30](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR30)]. The findings also highlight a rise in collaborative networks between Asian countries and global partners. These partnerships reflect an increasing awareness of the value of knowledge sharing and capacity enhancement within the realm of AIEdK-12. Such a cooperative approach plays a vital role not only in facilitating the dissemination of expertise but also in strengthening Asia’s influence and visibility in the international AIEdK-12 research community.

Among the top ten countries, five are from East Asia. It could be said that enhancing collaborative networks among diverse research institutions across different nations will be crucial for advancing future studies. Predictably, China, as an economic and technological power [[14](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR14)], emerges as the most productive nation in this field, having produced 180 articles and accumulated 1854 citations. This reflects the country’s robust research infrastructure and substantial funding [[44](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR44), [55](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR55)]. The notable citation impact can be linked to China’s strong focus on innovation and the integration of AI technology in K-12 environments. The country’s dominance may also be influenced by the fact that other Asian countries are investing less in AI compared to China [[56](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR56)]. Consequently, it is imperative for other Asian nations to intensify their efforts in advancing AI research, thereby contributing to the enhancement of the global standing in this field. This also underscores the urgency of implementing international capacity-building initiatives aimed at empowering developing regions to actively participate in advancing AI integration in educational settings. Strengthening partnerships between technologically advanced nations and underrepresented areas could promote broader relevance and inclusivity of AI technologies, ensuring equitable benefits across diverse educational contexts.

Undoubtedly, The Chinese University of Hong Kong stands out as the most prominent institution, followed by South China Normal University and The University of Hong Kong, demonstrating their major contributions to this research area. Findings from the present study indicate that a total of 531 articles have been published in 264 different journals. As the most prolific publication outlet, *Education and Information Technologies* (Q1) has received the highest number of citations and publications, highlighting its influence in the field. This trend can be explained by the fact that high-quality articles published in Q1 journals tend to attract more citations than those appearing in lower-quartile journals [[112](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR112)], underscoring their significant impact on the academic community. According to data from the Scopus database, the study by Hwang et al. [[61](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR61)] has been cited 174 times, demonstrating its substantial impact on research related to AIEdK-12. Furthermore, 1416 authors have contributed to the body of literature, with C.S. Chai from The Chinese University of Hong Kong emerging as the most influential researcher, and T.K.F. Chiu from the same institution recognized as the most productive scholar, reflecting their significant engagement in this domain. Based on the results, it is clear that pinpointing the key contributors, institutions, and scholarly outlets in AIEdK-12 research provides critical insights for guiding future strategic directions. Understanding which individuals, organizations, and publication venues are at the forefront of AI research in the Asian context enables stakeholders to promote collaborative efforts and facilitate the exchange of knowledge, thereby cultivating future leaders in this field.

Additionally, an analysis of the author’s keyword network highlights the breadth and diversity of AI applications in K-12 contexts. The findings show that the most frequently appearing keywords include “artificial intelligence,” “machine learning,” “AI education,” “deep learning,” and chatbot.” This reflects the increasing academic focus on this subject, particularly in recent years, and highlights significant interest in various trends related to AIEdK-12 [[4](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR4), [19](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR19), [32](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR32)]. For example, López-Chila et al. [[14](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR14)] recently conducted a bibliometric analysis using the Scopus database to examine the development and trends of artificial intelligence in higher education from 2017 to 2023. Although the study focused on the context of tertiary education, it also highlighted that keywords such as artificial intelligence, machine learning, ChatGPT, and deep learning constituted the core themes of the research. These keywords reflected the dominant areas of scholarly interest and offered a comprehensive overview of the intersection between AI and educational practices. Similarly, in bibliometric studies in the AIEd domain carried out by Talan [[113](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR113)] and Qin and Zhang [[13](/article/10.1007/s44163-025-00389-4#ref-CR13)], these keywords were identified as core nodes in the keyword network map.

Analyzing author keywords allows for the identification of publication clusters that explore connections between artificial intelligence, machine learning, deep learning, AI literacy, and ChatGPT from diverse perspectives. The emergence of keywords, such as ChatGPT, underscores the need to prepare teachers to be able to utilize the potential of this tool effectively in the future. Therefore, this study offers a comprehensive foundation for further research on AIEdK-12, benefiting teachers, researchers, policymakers, and practitioners in Asia. In summary, this study provides a comprehensive overview of AI research in K-12 education, highlighting key trends and identifying promising directions for future investigation. By exploring the development of AI in this context, this study advances our understanding of the transformative impact of AI on education and underscores the need for ongoing research and collaboration to advance teaching practices with cutting-edge AI technologies. The use of bibliometric analysis in AIEdK-12 offers valuable insights into the evolving relationship between technology and education, serving as a crucial resource for shaping future educational policies and practices. By examining citation patterns, collaboration networks, and keyword trends, researchers can obtain a thorough perspective on publication growth, major contributors, and the most influential works in this field.

### 4.2 Implications

This study presents both theoretical and practical implications relevant to the integration of AIEdK-12. From a theoretical perspective, it contributes to the existing body of literature by offering valuable insights into AIEdK-12 across Asia. These findings serve as a foundation for researchers who wish to explore this topic further using innovative methodologies. Additionally, this study provides essential information that can guide future research initiatives and inform decision-making in this field. On the practical side, this research examines emerging trends in AI applications at the K-12 level. Furthermore, the findings provide researchers with a general picture of this promising research field and hints for future research topics and directions. The outcomes provide a broad overview of this emerging field and suggest avenues for further exploration. Progress in AI integration within K-12 education demands more studies focusing on how students and teachers interact with AI tools in real classroom settings. Such efforts are essential to better understand how AI can be effectively leveraged to support teaching and learning processes. Accordingly, it is important to investigate a wider array of AI technologies and evaluate their pedagogical effectiveness in the context of K-12 education. Looking ahead, continuous enhancement of bibliometric analyses related to AI in K-12 settings could yield valuable insights, particularly in identifying annual trends in scholarly output and thematic focus. AI in K-12 education must remain a focal research area, attracting increased scholarly attention and leading to a greater number of peer-reviewed publications that contribute to both theoretical advancements and practical implementation.

Furthermore, the study emphasizes the significance of these findings for educators, policymakers, and other stakeholders. By leveraging the results, educators can integrate AI tools into classroom practices and design curricula that encourage creativity and equip students with the skills necessary for a future shaped by AI. Understanding how AI is currently being utilized by teachers and students can guide the development of instructional activities and professional development programs that promote effective use and foster improved learning outcomes. Given that AI literacy is becoming increasingly important, its inclusion in K-12 curricula is also recommended. Introducing students to foundational AI concepts dan practical applications in a responsible manner will not only enhance their digital competence but also prepare them for future academic and career endeavors. Thus, comprehensive research is required to determine the most effective strategies for embedding AI education within school curricula.

For policymakers, equally important is ensuring that teachers receive appropriate training in using AI tools and technologies within their instructional practices. This necessitates ongoing professional development programs that equip teachers with up-to-date knowledge and technical competencies. The successful deployment of AI in K-12 schools is also contingent on the availability of robust infrastructure, including quality hardware, software, stable internet access, and technical assistance. Schools must invest in specialized tools that support AI implementation, and policymakers have a key role in directing resources to ensure equitable access, particularly for underfunded schools. This focus on inclusivity underscores the transformative potential of AI technologies to reduce educational inequities and offer all students inclusive learning opportunities.

Moreover, the rapid adoption of AI in K-12 education necessitates ongoing research into emerging research trends, current status, and future direction. The study advocates for increased funding from governments and international organizations, particularly in developing countries, to support research and development efforts in this domain. Given the projected demand for AI-related job skills, investments in multidisciplinary educational interventions are crucial. Such initiatives may involve collaborations between educational institutions and technology firms, which can provide training opportunities and help ease financial constraints. Also, policy development should be informed by emerging trends in AI-related publications. Analyzing the trajectory of AIEdK-12 research can support strategic planning, resource allocation, and the formulation of policies to effectively integrate AI in school environments. In sum, advancing the use of AIEdK-12 requires collaborative efforts among researchers, educators, funding agencies, and policymakers. Interdisciplinary cooperation is essential for driving innovation and addressing real-world challenges associated with integrating AI into contemporary pedagogy.

5 Limitations
-------------

This study has certain limitations that should be recognized. Firstly, the analysis was primarily based on sources indexed in the Scopus database and only covered publications up to January 2025, which may not capture the full spectrum of AIEdK-12-related publications across Asia. As a result, studies published in local or regional outlets without international indexing, particularly those distributed offline, might have been overlooked, potentially leading to a partial depiction of the continent’s research landscape in this field. Future studies should broaden their data sources by incorporating additional databases, including region-specific and non-indexed journals. Expanding the data sources, such as incorporating articles from the Web of Science database, could also improve the comprehensiveness of the bibliometric analysis. Secondly, the study only considered English-language publications, which may lead to the exclusion of important contributions from non-English sources, particularly from non-English-speaking countries. The study’s exclusion of non-English publications could result in language bias, as pertinent findings reported in other languages, especially from countries where English is not the primary medium, may have been omitted. To address the limitation, expanding the inclusion criteria to consider multilingual publications will help minimize language bias and provide a more comprehensive overview of AIEdK-12 research activities across diverse linguistic and cultural contexts. Moreover, constraints may arise from the specific search query combinations and the timing of data retrieval, potentially affecting the sample of bibliometric articles. Additionally, this study focuses exclusively on articles published in Asian countries. Therefore, it is suggested that future research could benefit from comparisons with studies conducted in other regions or continents to gain a broader perspective on AIEdK-12. Lastly, grey literature, such as books, was not included in this study. As a result, relevant prior research presented in different document types may have been overlooked. Expanding the search criteria in future studies could help capture a wider range of relevant literature. Despite these limitations, this study offers valuable insights into the research trends on AIEdK-12. To the best of our knowledge, it is the first bibliometric analysis that explores the implementation of AIEdK-12 in Asia.
