# Effects of a human-robot collaborative teaching approach on preschoolers’ social-emotional competence and learning behaviors

## Abstract
Preschoolers' social-emotional competence is key to their long-term academic success. Developing this competence requires meaningful social interactions and emotional support, which the conventional teaching (CT) approach may not fully provide. This study proposed a human-robot collaborative teaching (HRCT) approach to facilitate preschoolers' social-emotional learning (SEL). A human-robot collaborative teaching system was developed to foster preschoolers' social-emotional competence through collaboration between the humanoid intelligent robot and the teacher. To evaluate the effectiveness of this approach, 96 preschoolers with an average age of 4.6 years were recruited. Of these, 44 were randomly assigned to the experimental group, which utilized the HRCT approach, while 52 were assigned to the control group, which followed the conventional teaching approach. The results indicated that the experimental group outperformed the control group in terms of social interaction skills, emotion comprehension skills, and interpersonal problem-solving skills. Significant differences were also observed in the learning behaviors of preschoolers during activities related to social interaction, emotion comprehension, and interpersonal problem solving. Further analysis of the teacher's perceptions and reflections revealed that, despite some limitations of the robot, its anthropomorphic and embodied features effectively sparked preschoolers' interest and engagement. These features contributed to enhancing their social-emotional competence by simulating and practicing SEL processes.
## Introduction
Social-emotional learning (SEL) is a vital approach to fostering the holistic development of children, with profound influences on their ability to recognize and regulate emotions and build healthy interpersonal relationships (Murano et al., 2020); this issue is thus gaining increasing attention from scholars (Martinez-Yarza et al., 2023). SEL focuses on the development of social-emotional competence, which mainly includes social interaction skills, emotion comprehension skills, and interpersonal problem-solving skills. This competence can help children engage in positive social interactions, effectively identify and manage their emotions, and solve the interpersonal problems they may encounter (Osher et al., 2016; Steed et al., 2021).
Scholars have indicated the challenges of developing children's SEL (Martinez-Yarza et al., 2023). Accordingly, several previous studies have attempted to situate children in technological contexts, such as by using tablets (Kahveci et al., 2023), virtual reality (Phillips, Jarden, & Bowles, 2025), wearable devices (Dechsling et al., 2021), and robots (Chernyak & Gary, 2019), to address this problem; unfortunately, the results were not as good as expected. Researchers have pointed out that existing digital tools generally offer limited support for authentic SEL due to their single interaction mode and restricted emotional expression (Yang, 2024). For example, although traditional robots introduce basic interactivity, they still lack embodiment and natural emotional responsiveness, making it difficult to facilitate deep social-emotional experiences (Chernyak & Gary, 2019).
With advances in artificial intelligence (AI), AI tools have attracted widespread attention (Karaoglan Yilmaz & Yilmaz, 2025; Yilmaz et al., 2024). Some scholars have increasingly turned to humanoid intelligent robots, which combine embodied interaction with adaptive intelligence to foster children's social-emotional competence (Crompton et al., 2018; Wu & Li, 2024). These robots can simulate diverse social scenarios and provide personalized support aligned with children's needs, indicating their potential to enhance SEL in preschool settings (Kim & Tscholl, 2021). Teachers can further utilize such robots to guide interactions, offer emotional scaffolding, and support conflict resolution in real time (Benvenuti & Mazzoni, 2019). However, although researchers have attempted to explore the potential of robots as tutors for fostering preschoolers' social-emotional competence (Smakman et al., 2022), how teachers and humanoid intelligent robots can best work together as collaborative agents to jointly support the development of preschoolers' social-emotional competence remains an open issue. It is expected that, while serving as an effective collaborator for teachers, humanoid intelligent robots could undertake routine instructional tasks, providing preschoolers with timely multimodal feedback, as well as promoting the development of their social-emotional competence through experiential contexts. This would allow teachers to focus on personalized guidance, emotional support, and classroom management, and hence maximize the effectiveness of human-robot collaboration (Crompton et al., 2018; Hu, 2023; Qidwai et al., 2019). Therefore, this study proposed a human-robot collaborative teaching (HRCT) approach to develop preschoolers' social-emotional competence. To assess its effectiveness, this study applied this approach to an SEL course for preschoolers to address the following research questions.
- RQ1
Can the HRCT approach improve preschoolers' social interaction skills compared to the conventional teaching (CT) approach?
- RQ2
Can the HRCT approach improve preschoolers' emotion comprehension skills compared to the CT approach?
- RQ3
Can the HRCT approach improve preschoolers' interpersonal problem-solving skills compared to the CT approach?
- RQ4
What are the differences in the learning behavior patterns of preschoolers adopting the HRCT approach and those adopting the CT approach?
- RQ5
Based on observations of preschoolers' learning behaviors during the activities, what were the teacher's perceptions of and reflections on the use of the HRCT/CT approaches?

SEL is defined as the process whereby preschoolers gradually develop and apply core competences related to social and emotional development as they navigate complex scenarios throughout their growth (Yang et al., 2019). The Collaborative for Academic, Social, and Emotional Learning (CASEL) model is often used to guide analyses of preschoolers' social-emotional competence (Vinal & Renshaw, 2024), but there are significant differences in how this competence is measured in evaluations.

The framework aimed to facilitate social interaction, emotion comprehension, and interpersonal problem solving in four stages: concrete experience, reflective observation, abstract conceptualization, and active experimentation.
