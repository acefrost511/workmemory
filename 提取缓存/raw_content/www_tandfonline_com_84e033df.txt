## Abstract
### Purpose
This study presents a comprehensive literature review examining the role of artificial intelligence (AI) in autism education. It explores how AI technologies—such as adaptive learning systems, natural language processing–based communication aids, emotion recognition algorithms, socially assistive robots, and immersive augmented and virtual reality platforms—support teaching, learning, and behavioral development for autistic individuals.
### Methods
Peer-reviewed literature was critically synthesized to identify trends in AI tool development, including adaptive learning systems, natural language processing–based communication aids, emotion recognition algorithms, socially assistive robots, and immersive augmented and virtual reality platforms. Emphasis was placed on studies integrating co-design methodologies involving educators, therapists, families, and autistic learners.
### Results
Findings reveal that AI enables personalized learning, enhances social and emotional skill development, and promotes inclusive educational environments. Co-design practices improve usability, accessibility, and cultural sensitivity in tool development. However, limitations include the scarcity of large, diverse datasets, challenges in gathering longitudinal multimodal data, limited cross-cultural validation, and insufficient focus on hardware innovation relative to software solutions.
### Conclusions
AI tools hold transformative potential for autism education, yet equitable and effective implementation requires addressing data diversity, ethical oversight, and interdisciplinary collaboration.
## IMPLICATIONS FOR REHABILITATION
\* Collaborative Development: Active engagement among AI developers, educators, therapists, and autistic individuals ensures technologies meet real-world rehabilitation needs.
\* Personalised Intervention: AI tools enable adaptive, data-informed support that enhances communication, learning, and behavioural outcomes in autism education.
\* Sustained Impact: Longitudinal integration of AI systems fosters continuous progress tracking and enduring developmental benefits.
\* Equitable Access: User-centred design and affordability considerations expand accessibility across diverse abilities and educational settings.
\* Ethical and Safe Use: Transparent algorithms and robust data protection uphold trust, safeguarding autistic learners’ privacy and wellbeing.

### Notes on contributors
#### Oyeyemi Patricia Adako
\*\*\*Oyeyemi Patricia Adako\*\*\* is the Executive Director of the Education for Autism Initiative, bringing over a decade of experience in instructional design and pedagogy. She focuses on creating technology-enhanced, student-centered learning environments for diverse learners, especially those on the autism spectrum. A strong advocate for inclusivity and personalized instruction, Mrs. Adako highlights the role of artificial intelligence (AI) in enhancing special education. Her research has resulted in several peer-reviewed articles on the potential of AI to enhance educational outcomes for autistic students. She holds a Master of Arts in Education from Anglia Ruskin University.
#### Oluwafemi Clement Adeusi
\*\*\*Oluwafemi Clement Adeusi\*\*\* holds an MSc in Computer Science (Networks and Security) and a Diploma in Data Analysis from Staffordshire University, United Kingdom, and a B.Tech in Computer Science from Olusegun Agagu University of Science and Technology, Nigeria. His research focuses on artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and digital transformation, with particular emphasis on inclusive education and assistive technologies. He has published in several peer-reviewed journals and is a member of NIPES, ISIE, and IMC-Nigeria. Oluwafemi also holds professional certifications including CCNA, CompTIA Network+, and CNSS. His work bridges technology, education, and community development through innovation and collaboration.
#### Peter Adeniyi Alaba
\*\*\*Peter Adeniyi Alaba\*\*\*, a renowned sustainability researcher at the University of Malaya, Malaysia, is recognized for his work in renewable and sustainable energy, as well as sustainable wastewater treatment. His pioneering advancements in multidisciplinary and interdisciplinary research have garnered immense recognition. Dr. Alaba’s influence, however, extends beyond his scientific achievements. His advocacy for AI integration in autism education, as evidenced by his co-authorship of key works on how AI can personalize learning for children with autism, and his multidisciplinary approach, continues to shape and influence both the scientific and educational technology communities.
