2026 Youth Report: Lead with youth
==================================

2026 Youth ReportDownload the report

* [English](https://unesdoc.unesco.org/ark:/48223/pf0000396964_eng)

The 2026 Youth Report "Lead with youth" is the result of a partnership with the United Nations Youth Office to understand the leading role of youth and students in education decision-making around the world. Building on the momentum of the Youth Declaration adopted at the 2022 Transforming Education Summit- which recognized young people as active agents of systemic and long-term change- this report presents a first attempt to measure youth and student participation in education decision-making based on an analysis of the data collected through surveys to governments and nation-wide youth and student organizations.

This report serves a dual purpose at a critical juncture for global education. It concludes the [2024/5 GEM Report cycle of reports on leadership](https://www.unesco.org/gem-report/en/publication/leadership) with a focus on the importance of youth and student leaders in education, while simultaneously acting as the first in a new [Countdown to 2030 series](https://www.unesco.org/gem-report/en/publication/equity-and-access) by the GEM Report that will examine the shape of education beyond the current SDG framework. By documenting the diverse mechanisms nations employ to frame youth and student participation, this report aims to empower young people in the dissemination of and debates around these findings. More fundamentally, it advocates for the essential inclusion of youth and student voices as we enter the final years of SDG 4 and begin conceptualizing a new global education agenda—one that must be shaped not for youth, but with them.

Accompanying this report will be a mapping of more than 500 nation-wide youth and student organizations involved in education decision-making globally and a series of advocacy and communication outputs co-designed with youth.

The report will be launched on 23 January 2026 during a high-level event to celebrate [International Day of Education](https://www.unesco.org/en/days/education) at UNESCO's headquarters in Paris.

2026 Youth Report reveals a gap in youth participation in education decision-making
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A new global report released on International Day of Education analyzing youth engagement in education decision-making reveals a troubling disconnect: while formal consultation mechanisms with young people exist in many countries, these structures seldom translate into genuine influence over education policies that will shape their futures.

The report [*Lead with youth*](https://www.unesco.org/gem-report/en/publication/2026youthreport?hub=66580) released by the UNESCO Global Education Monitoring Report, in partnership with the UN Youth Office, is a response to the [2022 Youth Declaration](https://www.un.org/en/transforming-education-summit/youth-declaration) of the [Transforming Education Summit](https://www.un.org/en/transforming-education-summit/youth-declaration). It provides the first global baseline assessment of how governments engage youth and student organizations in education legislation and policymaking.
